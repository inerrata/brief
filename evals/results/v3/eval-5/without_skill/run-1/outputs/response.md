# Google Responsive Search Ads (RSA) — Character Limits & Asset Counts

## Character limits

| Asset | Character limit |
|---|---|
| Headline | **30 characters** each |
| Description | **90 characters** each |
| Display path (2 optional fields) | **15 characters** each |

## How many you can load in

| Asset | Minimum required | Maximum allowed |
|---|---|---|
| Headlines | 3 | **15** |
| Descriptions | 2 | **4** |

## What actually shows in the ad

Google mixes and matches your assets, so the served ad shows **up to 3 headlines** and **up to 2 descriptions** at a time (sometimes fewer — e.g., 2 headlines and 1 description on smaller screens). You can't control which combinations appear unless you use **pinning** (locking an asset to Headline position 1/2/3 or Description position 1/2), though pinning reduces the combinations Google can test and typically lowers your Ad Strength rating.

## Quick practical tips

- **Max out your assets** (15 headlines, 4 descriptions) — more variety improves Ad Strength and gives the algorithm more combinations to optimize.
- **Front-load value** in the first ~3 headlines and first description, since those are most likely to serve together.
- **Make every headline standalone** — any headline can pair with any other, so avoid headlines that only make sense in sequence.
- **Character counts include spaces and punctuation.** Note that ad customizers (like countdown timers or dynamic keyword insertion) can let rendered text exceed the limit, but the static text you type must fit within it.
- Keep at least a few headlines **unpinned and varied** (mix keywords, benefits, CTAs, and proof points like "Free Shipping" or "Rated 4.8/5").

**TL;DR:** 15 headlines at 30 characters each, 4 descriptions at 90 characters each, plus two 15-character display path fields. Minimum to launch: 3 headlines and 2 descriptions.
