Happy to write this — and a positioning statement is exactly the kind of thing worth getting right, because everything downstream (your homepage, pitch, ads) inherits from it. A guessed one reads as fact and quietly steers all of that wrong, so let me ask three quick things first. "Scheduling software for clinics" tells me the *category*, but not the parts that make positioning actually differentiated:

1. **Who exactly is this for — the narrowest core buyer?** "Clinics" spans a lot. Solo private practices? Multi-location dental or physiotherapy groups? Large hospital outpatient departments? And who actually buys/champions it — a practice manager, a clinic owner, front-desk staff? (e.g. "independent dental practices with 2–10 chairs," not "clinics.")

2. **What's the one thing you do that the main alternatives don't?** The genuine differentiator — automated patient reminders that cut no-shows, online self-booking, EHR/insurance integration, fast setup, price? Position on the dimension where you actually win *and* the buyer actually chooses.

3. **Who or what are you really competing against?** Often it's not a rival app — it's a paper appointment book, a generic tool like Calendly, the booking module bundled into their existing practice-management system, or just the phone-and-spreadsheet status quo. The right "unlike…" depends on this.

Answer those three and I'll write a tight, defensible statement (plus a one-line value-prop version for your homepage).
