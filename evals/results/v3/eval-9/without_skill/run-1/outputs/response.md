# Brightline Homepage — Hero Section

Here's a hero section written in your voice — plainspoken, warm, and human. I've included the core version plus a couple of swap-in options so you can pick what feels most like you.

---

## Recommended version

**Eyebrow:**
Bookkeeping for independent restaurants

**Headline:**
You didn't open a restaurant to do paperwork.

**Subhead:**
Brightline handles your books for you — start to finish. No spreadsheets, no late nights catching up on receipts. Just clean numbers you can actually trust, handled by real people who get restaurants.

**Proof line:**
Our clients get back an average of 11 hours a month. That's time back on the floor, in the kitchen, or finally off your feet. *(2025 customer survey, n=142)*

**Primary button:**
See how it works

**Secondary button:**
Talk to a real person

---

## Why these choices

- **The headline leads with the feeling, not the feature.** Restaurant owners don't wake up wanting "bookkeeping" — they want the paperwork gone. So the hook is the relief, and the "done-for-you" promise lives right underneath it.
- **The 11-hours stat is the emotional payoff, not just a number.** Instead of "save 11 hours," we say what those hours are actually *for*. That's what makes it land.
- **No "leverage," no "solutions," no "streamline."** Reads the way you'd actually talk to an owner across the bar after close.
- **"Real people who get restaurants" / "Talk to a real person"** leans into the done-for-you, human side — which is probably your biggest edge over the DIY apps.

## One thing to flag for you

I assumed the hero should sell the **relief / time-back** angle over, say, price or tax accuracy — because that's what your stat supports best and what tends to hook owners fastest. If your bigger differentiator is actually something else (avoiding costly tax mistakes, restaurant-specific expertise, a flat predictable price), tell me and I'll re-cut the headline around that instead. Quick gut-check questions if you want it tighter:

1. Who's the hero buyer — solo owner-operator, or someone with a small team and a manager?
2. Is "11 hours a month" your strongest proof point, or do you have a money/savings number you'd rather lead with?
3. Any specific CTA your site already uses (e.g., "Book a call," "Get a quote") that I should match?
