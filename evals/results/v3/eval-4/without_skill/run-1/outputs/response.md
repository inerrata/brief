# Naming your Monday-morning metrics email

Quick gut-check on "PulseIQ" first, then a set of alternatives organized by the kind of feeling you want the name to create.

## The honest take on "PulseIQ"

It's not bad — "Pulse" nicely evokes a regular, rhythmic health check, which fits a recurring summary. But it has two problems:

1. **"PulseIQ" is crowded.** "Pulse" is one of the most-used words in analytics/monitoring product naming, and the "...IQ" suffix is a well-worn SaaS cliché (LeadIQ, SalesIQ, DataIQ, etc.). It will feel generic and may create trademark/SEO collision headaches.
2. **The "IQ" oversells.** "IQ" implies intelligence/scoring. Your feature is a *digest* — it summarizes existing metrics. If it doesn't actually rank, score, or recommend, "IQ" writes a check the product doesn't cash, and users notice.

So: keep the instinct (rhythm, vitality), drop the suffix-cliché.

## A naming decision to make first

The biggest naming choice isn't the words — it's the **strategy**. Pick one:

- **Descriptive** (says what it is): easiest to understand, lowest marketing lift, hardest to trademark/own. Good if discoverability inside your app matters more than brand.
- **Evocative** (suggests a feeling): more memorable and ownable, needs a tiny bit of context the first time. Good if this becomes a feature users talk about.

My recommendation: lean evocative-but-clear, because a named ritual ("Did you read your ___ this morning?") is far stickier than a described one.

## Options

### Tier 1 — Recommended (clear + ownable)

| Name | Why it works | Watch-out |
|---|---|---|
| **Monday Brief** | Says exactly when and what. Zero learning curve. "Brief" implies short and skimmable — sets the right expectation. | Very descriptive, so hard to trademark broadly. |
| **The Monday Memo** | Same clarity, slightly warmer/editorial tone. Reads like something a person sends you. | Ties you to Monday forever (fine if that's the promise). |
| **Weekly Pulse** | Keeps your "pulse" instinct, ditches the IQ cliché. Honest about cadence, evokes a health check. | "Pulse" is common — check for in-market collisions. |
| **Kickoff** | Frames the email as the thing that *starts* the work week. Energetic, ownable, not metric-jargon. | Slightly less explicit that it contains data. |

### Tier 2 — Evocative (more brand, needs one line of context)

- **Daybreak** — your week's metrics, first thing. Optimistic, fresh.
- **First Light** — same idea, a touch more premium.
- **Compass** — the read that points you at what matters this week. (Good if you later add recommendations — earns the "intelligence" framing honestly.)
- **The Readout** — borrows from mission-control/lab culture; signals "here are your numbers, clearly."
- **Signal** — what cut through the noise this week. (Common word — verify availability.)

### Tier 3 — Descriptive (lowest risk, lowest distinctiveness)

- **Weekly Summary** / **Monday Summary**
- **Metrics Digest** / **The Digest**
- **Your Week in Numbers**

These are safe and instantly understood. Use one of these if the feature should feel like a utility, not a brand.

## My pick

If I had to ship one: **Monday Brief** as the product name, with **"Your week, before your first coffee"** (or similar) as the tagline. It's self-explanatory, sets the skimmable-and-short expectation, and the ritual framing ("read your Monday Brief") makes it easy for users and your own marketing to talk about. Use **Weekly Pulse** as the fallback if you want to preserve the original "pulse" equity.

If the roadmap includes real scoring/recommendations soon, name it **Compass** now so the name grows into the intelligence story instead of overclaiming today.

## Before you lock it in — 4 quick checks

1. **Does the name still hold if you change the day or cadence?** "Monday Brief" breaks if you later send it Sundays or daily. If cadence might change, prefer **Weekly Pulse**, **Kickoff**, **Daybreak**, or **Compass**.
2. **Trademark + domain + in-app collision:** quick search for existing analytics products using the name, and check whether you need a separate domain or just an in-app label.
3. **Say it out loud in a sentence:** "I check my **Kickoff** every Monday." If it sounds natural as a ritual, it's a keeper.
4. **Truth-in-naming:** only use "IQ," "AI," "Smart," or "Insights" if the feature actually analyzes/recommends. For a straight digest, don't.

## A couple of questions that would sharpen this

- Is this strictly a **summary/digest**, or does it (or will it soon) surface anomalies, trends, or recommendations? That decides whether an "intelligence" name like *Compass* is honest.
- Is **Monday** a permanent promise, or could the cadence change? That decides whether to bake the day into the name.
- Who's the primary reader — an exec wanting the headline, or an operator wanting the detail? That nudges tone (e.g., *Brief/Readout* for execs, *Pulse/Digest* for operators).
