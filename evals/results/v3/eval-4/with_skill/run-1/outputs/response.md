Quick read on "PulseIQ" first, then a batch of alternatives across different naming angles.

*Assuming this is a feature name inside your existing analytics product (not a standalone brand), aimed at your current users — business/ops people who already log in. Adjust if it's actually a new top-line product.*

## The honest take on "PulseIQ"

It's not bad, but it has two problems worth flagging:

1. **It's crowded.** "Pulse" is one of the most over-used words in analytics/HR/monitoring tooling (Pulse surveys, Pulse dashboards, etc.), and "IQ" is a tired suffix that's been bolted onto hundreds of B2B products. Together they read as generic SaaS — the name a tool gets when marketing wants it to *sound* smart rather than *say* what it does.
2. **It describes the wrong thing.** The actual value here isn't "intelligence" — it's that the report **comes to you, on a schedule, without you asking.** "PulseIQ" hides the one genuinely useful thing about the feature: it's automatic and weekly. A good feature name should let a user guess what it does before they click it.

So before you commit, run it through three quick tests:
- **The repeat test** — would a user remember "PulseIQ" vs. just calling it "the Monday email"? (They'll call it the Monday email.)
- **The describe test** — does the name hint at what it does? "PulseIQ" doesn't.
- **The crowding test** — search the name in your space. If five competitors have something Pulse-ish, you're not differentiating, you're blending in.

## What the name actually needs to convey

The feature is doing three things, and the best names lean into one of them:
- **Cadence** — it's recurring and timed (weekly, Monday morning).
- **Delivery** — it arrives to you; you don't go fetch it.
- **Synthesis** — it's a summary of your key metrics, not raw data.

My honest recommendation: lean toward **cadence/delivery**, because that's the differentiated, true thing. "Summary of your metrics" is table stakes; "shows up every Monday so you start the week informed" is the hook.

## Options, by angle

**A. Descriptive — says exactly what it is (lowest risk, highest clarity)**
These win on the describe test and need almost no explaining. Best if you want adoption over flash.
- **Monday Brief** — clear, ownable internally, and it matches what users will *already* call it. Strong default.
- **Weekly Brief**
- **Metrics Digest**
- **The Monday Report**
- **Weekly Recap**

**B. Cadence-led — leans on the rhythm/ritual**
Good if you want the feature to feel like a habit users look forward to.
- **Monday Pulse** (keeps your "pulse" instinct but grounds it in the cadence — far better than PulseIQ)
- **Cadence**
- **Weekly Wrap**
- **The Standup** (nods to the Monday team ritual; fits if your audience is product/eng)
- **Kickoff** (the report that kicks off your week)

**C. Delivery-led — emphasizes "it comes to you"**
Good if the auto-delivery is the thing you most want to advertise.
- **Inbox Insights**
- **Delivered** (as a feature label: "Reports, delivered")
- **Dispatch** — has a "sent to you on a schedule" connotation; distinctive and short.
- **The Drop** (your weekly metrics drop — more consumer/energetic)

**D. Synthesis-led — emphasizes the "key metrics, summarized" value**
- **Signal** (the summary cuts noise down to what matters — clean, but "signal" is also fairly common)
- **Highlights**
- **TL;DR** (cheeky; "your week's metrics, TL;DR" — only if your brand voice can carry it)

## My shortlist

If I had to pick, in order:
1. **Monday Brief** — clearest, most memorable, matches user language, zero explanation needed.
2. **Dispatch** — distinctive, captures the scheduled-delivery angle, room to grow beyond Mondays.
3. **Monday Pulse** — if leadership is attached to "Pulse," this keeps the equity while fixing the generic-ness.

I'd steer away from PulseIQ unless there's existing brand equity in "Pulse" you haven't mentioned.

## One thing to decide that changes the answer

Is this locked to **Monday/weekly forever**, or might it become configurable (daily, monthly, on-demand)? It matters:
- If it's **always Monday/weekly** → a literal name like *Monday Brief* is perfect and reinforces the ritual.
- If it might become **flexible cadence** → avoid baking "Monday" or "Weekly" in, or you'll be stuck renaming. Go with *Dispatch*, *Cadence*, or *Brief* instead.

## Before you ship the name

Naming directions are easy; clearing them is the hard part.
