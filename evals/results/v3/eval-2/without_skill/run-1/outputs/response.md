# BrightBooks vs. QuickBooks — Sales Battle Card

**Use this when:** A prospect is evaluating, currently using, or threatening to choose QuickBooks. Built for ecommerce small-brand deals. Keep it on a second screen during discovery and demo calls.

> **One-line positioning:** *BrightBooks is bookkeeping built for ecommerce — QuickBooks is general-purpose accounting you have to bend, bolt onto, and babysit to make it work for an online store.*

---

| | **BrightBooks** | **QuickBooks (Online)** |

## 2. Why We Win (lead with these)

1. **Ecommerce-native, not adapted.** We were built for online brands from day one. Orders, marketplace fees, payment-processor deductions, refunds, and payouts map automatically to the right accounts. QuickBooks treats a Shopify or Amazon payout as a single lump deposit — so margins, fees, and COGS get muddy unless you buy and configure a separate connector.

2. **You don't need to be an accountant.** BrightBooks speaks in plain ecommerce terms (revenue, fees, COGS, profit per channel). QuickBooks speaks in chart-of-accounts and journal entries — most founders end up paying a bookkeeper just to keep it clean.

3. **Real ecommerce numbers, automatically.** True product/channel-level margins, fee breakdowns, and cash-vs-payout reconciliation come standard. In QuickBooks these are bolt-on workarounds.

4. **Fast, low-maintenance setup.** Connect the store and your numbers populate. No multi-week implementation, no consultant.

5. **Predictable pricing.** No surprise jump to a higher tier the moment you need a real ecommerce report.

---

## 3. Where QuickBooks Is Strong (don't pretend otherwise)

Acknowledge these honestly — credibility wins deals. Then reframe.

- **Accountant familiarity / ecosystem.** Nearly every bookkeeper knows QuickBooks. -> *Reframe: That's exactly the cost — you're optimizing for your accountant's convenience, not your store's clarity. BrightBooks is built so you understand your own numbers, and it exports clean files any accountant can use at tax time.*
- **Breadth of general features** (payroll add-ons, lending, broad integrations). -> *Reframe: Breadth you'll never use for an online store, at the price of ecommerce depth you need every day.*
- **Brand trust / market share.** "Nobody got fired for buying QuickBooks." -> *Reframe: Market share with general SMBs. For ecommerce specifically, the question is whether it actually shows you per-channel profit without three add-ons and a consultant.*

## 4. Objection Handling

| Prospect says… | You respond… |
|---|---|
| **"QuickBooks is the standard / my accountant uses it."** | "Totally — and BrightBooks exports clean, accountant-ready files, so they still get what they need at tax time. The difference is *you* get ecommerce-native reporting day to day, instead of paying someone to translate QuickBooks for you." |
| **"It's cheaper / I already pay for it."** | "Once you add the connector you need for ecommerce (A2X, Synder, etc.) plus the bookkeeper time to keep it clean, QuickBooks usually costs *more* all-in. We're one tool, one predictable price, ecommerce ready out of the box." |
| **"QuickBooks does way more."** | "It does — most of it for businesses that aren't online stores. For your brand, more general features means more setup, more clutter, and the ecommerce reports you actually need are still missing or add-ons." |
| **"Switching is a pain."** | "We handle the migration and import your history. Most brands are up and reconciling in a day, not the weeks a QuickBooks rollout takes." |
| **"Will it scale as I grow?"** | "Yes — and it scales the way ecommerce grows: more channels, more SKUs, more order volume. That's exactly the dimension QuickBooks struggles with without add-ons." |

## 5. Discovery Questions (surface our advantages)

- "When a Shopify/Amazon payout hits, how do you currently split out the platform fees, refunds, and actual revenue?"
- "Can you see profit *per channel* and *per product* right now, or do you have to build that yourself?"
- "How much are you spending on connectors or a bookkeeper on top of QuickBooks?"
- "How long did QuickBooks take to set up — and who set it up?"
- "How confident are you that your COGS and margins are accurate today?"

If they hesitate on any of these, that's your wedge.

## 8. Trap to Avoid (for the rep)

- **Don't trash QuickBooks broadly** — it's a respected product; bashing it reads as insecure. Stay specific to *ecommerce* gaps.
- **Don't get pulled into a feature-count war** — you lose on breadth, you win on fit. Always steer back to "built for ecommerce."
- **Don't over-claim** — if a feature isn't shipped, say "on the roadmap," not "we do that."

---

## 9. The 30-Second Pitch (memorize this)

> "QuickBooks is great general accounting software — but you don't run a general business, you run an ecommerce brand. BrightBooks is built specifically for online stores: your orders, fees, payouts, and COGS sync automatically, so you see real per-channel profit without a connector, a consultant, or an accounting degree. You'll be set up today, and your accountant still gets clean files at tax time. The only thing you lose by switching is the bookkeeper bill."
