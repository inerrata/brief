## Competitor snapshot
- **What it is:** QuickBooks Online (Intuit) — the default general-purpose accounting/bookkeeping suite. Huge brand, built for *every* small business, not ecommerce specifically.
- **Who they sell to:** Everyone — freelancers, restaurants, contractors, agencies, retailers. Ecommerce is one of many verticals, not the focus.
- **Pricing posture:** Tiered SaaS (Simple Start → Plus → Advanced), `~$[X]–$[Y]/mo` list, near-constant promo discounts (often 50% for 3 months). Ecommerce reconciliation (Shopify, Amazon, payouts, fees, sales tax across channels) typically requires **add-ons or third-party connectors** (e.g. A2X-style tools), which raises the real total cost. *Verify current tier pricing before quoting — Intuit changes it often.*

## Where they win  *(say it honestly — reps who get blindsided stop trusting the card)*

- **Brand & trust.** Every accountant knows QuickBooks; some buyers won't consider anything else, and their bookkeeper may insist on it.
- **Breadth.** Payroll, deep invoicing, mileage, full GL, an enormous app marketplace, and a large accountant network. If the buyer needs general accounting beyond ecommerce bookkeeping, QB covers more surface area.
- **Ecosystem lock-in.** Existing QB users have historical data, a bookkeeper trained on it, and switching feels risky at tax time.

**Qualify out** when: the prospect needs full payroll + general accounting more than ecommerce reconciliation, or their accountant flatly mandates QuickBooks and won't budge. Don't burn cycles there.
