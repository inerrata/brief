# brand.md

## Company
- **Name:** Kindling
- **What it is (one sentence):** Email marketing built for indie fiction authors.
- **Industry:** Author tools / email marketing
- **Stage:** growth
- **Main alternative customers would use instead:** Mailchimp, or no list at all
- **Single most important differentiator:** templates and automations designed around book launches, not ecommerce

## Audience
- **Primary audience (be narrow):** self-published fiction authors with 1k-50k readers
- **Their biggest pain:** launches flop because their list is cold and generic tools feel built for shops, not stories
- **What they believe now → what we want them to believe:** "email marketing is salesy and not for writers" → "my list is my most loyal readership and Kindling makes it feel like writing, not selling"

## Voice & tone
- **Adjectives that describe our voice:** warm, bookish, playful
- **Never:** never call an email send a 'blast'; no growth-hacker jargon; no exclamation-mark pileups
- **Formality (1 = casual, 5 = formal):** 2

## Proof on file
- **Stats:** Kindling users average a 52% open rate vs the 21% industry average (internal data 2025, n=3,400 accounts)
- **Testimonials:** "Kindling doubled my launch-week sales." — Mia Torres, romance author (permission on file)

## Standing constraints
- **Channels we use:** email, Instagram
- **Current goal this quarter:** free-to-paid upgrades
