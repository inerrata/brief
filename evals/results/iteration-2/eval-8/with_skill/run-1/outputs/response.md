## What to post on LinkedIn for your consulting business

The goal of LinkedIn content for consultants is to make the right people think "this person gets my problem" — so when they need help, you're already top of mind. Here's what actually works:

---

### The content mix (roughly)

- **~60% — Teach something specific.** Share insight from your actual work. Not generic advice — the specific, hard-won kind that makes someone stop scrolling.
- **~25% — Build credibility and trust.** Case studies (anonymized if needed), results you've driven, process you use, behind-the-scenes on how you think.
- **~15% — Direct ask or signal availability.** "Taking on two clients in Q3" or "If you're dealing with X, here's how I'd help."

---

### Post types that perform well for consultants

**1. The "here's what I actually see" observation**
Lead with something you notice constantly in your client work that most people don't say out loud. These build authority fast.

*Example hook:* "Most companies think they have a sales problem. They actually have a prioritization problem. Here's the difference..."

**2. The short framework or checklist**
Give away a simple framework you use. It sounds counterintuitive, but giving away your thinking makes people want to hire you — they see how you think, and they realize doing it themselves is still hard.

*Example hook:* "The 3-question audit I run in every first client call."

**3. The mini case study**
One client problem, what you found, what you did, what changed. Keep it concrete. You don't need to name the client.

*Example hook:* "A client came to me with a 40% churn rate. Here's what we found in week one."

**4. The counterintuitive take**
One thing you believe that most people in your space get wrong. This earns engagement and signals genuine expertise.

*Example hook:* "Stop leading with deliverables in your proposals. Do this instead."

**5. The honest process post**
How you actually work. What a first engagement looks like. What you won't do. These help prospects self-qualify and reduce friction.

---

### LinkedIn mechanics that matter

- **Hook is everything.** The first 1–2 lines determine whether someone clicks "see more." Lead with the payoff, not the context.
- **Short lines, white space.** LinkedIn rewards scannable posts. One idea per line where possible.
- **Post 2–3x per week** — consistency beats frequency. One great post beats five mediocre ones.
- **End with something, not nothing.** A question ("What's your experience?"), a soft CTA ("If this resonates, I work with [type of company] on [type of problem]"), or just a strong final line.

---

### What not to post

- Generic motivational content ("Monday mindset...") — erodes credibility for consultants.
- Vague service announcements with no specifics.
- Engagement-bait polls that have nothing to do with your work.
