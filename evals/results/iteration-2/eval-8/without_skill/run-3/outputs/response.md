Here's what actually works on LinkedIn for consultants — based on what builds audience, generates inbound, and earns referrals rather than just collecting likes.

---

## What to Post on LinkedIn for Your Consulting Business

### The goal
LinkedIn content for consultants has one job: make the right people think "this person understands my problem" — so when they're ready to hire, you're already the obvious choice.

---

### The content mix

- **~60% — Specific insights from your work.** Not generic business advice. The kind of thing you only know because you've been in the room. This builds credibility faster than anything else.
- **~25% — Proof and process.** Mini case studies, how you think, what a real engagement looks like, outcomes you've achieved (even anonymized).
- **~15% — Direct signal of availability.** "Taking on two clients in Q3." "Working with [type of company] on [specific problem] — if that's you, let's talk."

---

### 5 post types that consistently perform

**1. The observation post**
Something you notice over and over in client work that most people don't say out loud. Short, specific, stops the scroll.

*Example:* "Most [type of company] don't have a [problem] problem. They have a [reframed problem] problem. Here's the difference..."

**2. The framework post**
Give away a simple 3-step or checklist framework you actually use. This feels counterintuitive but it works — people see how you think, want to hire you to apply it, and realize doing it alone is harder than it looks.

*Example:* "The 4 questions I ask in every first client conversation."

**3. The mini case study**
One problem a client had, what you found, what you changed, what happened. Concrete numbers where possible. No name needed.

*Example:* "A client came to me with a team that was busy but not productive. Here's the one change we made..."

**4. The contrarian take**
One thing most people in your space get wrong. This earns respect and separates you from generic advisors.

*Example:* "Stop optimizing your onboarding. It's not what's killing retention."

**5. The process transparency post**
What working with you actually looks like. What you don't do. What you need from a client to do good work. These help the right clients self-select in — and the wrong ones self-select out.

---

### The mechanics that matter

- **First 1–2 lines = everything.** LinkedIn cuts off at "...see more." Your hook has to earn the click. Lead with the payoff, not the context.
- **Short lines and white space.** Dense paragraphs get skipped. One thought per line.
- **2–3 posts per week** is the right cadence for one person. Sustainable beats impressive-then-silent.
- **End with purpose.** A question, a soft CTA, or a strong closing line. Never just trail off.

---

### What to avoid

- Motivational content unrelated to your work ("It's Monday, let's go!") — it erodes professional credibility.
- Vague service posts with no specifics ("Excited to share that I help companies grow!").
- Engagement bait ("What do you think? Comment below!") with no real substance.
