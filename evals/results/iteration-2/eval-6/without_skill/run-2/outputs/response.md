Here are three value proposition options for your meal-prep service — pick the one that fits your brand tone, or use them as a starting point:

---

**Option 1 — The contrast angle**
> Real food on the table in 5 minutes. No ultra-processed ingredients. No hour in the kitchen.

*Use this as a homepage headline or ad tagline.*

---

**Option 2 — The outcome angle**
> We help busy parents get a real, home-cooked meal on the table every night — even the nights when there's no time to cook.

*Use this as a subheadline or pitch.*

---

**Option 3 — The direct benefit angle**
> Dinner in 5 minutes. No junk. No stress.

*Use this for social bios, short ads, or packaging.*

---

**What makes these work:**
- They lead with the parent's situation (busy, no time) rather than your product features.
- "5 minutes" is the standout concrete claim — keep it front and center everywhere.
- "No ultra-processed ingredients" signals trust and differentiates you from fast food, meal kits that cut corners, and convenience food in general.

The strongest one for a homepage hero is Option 1 — it covers both your core differentiators (speed and quality) in one punchy line.
