Before I build your brand voice guide, I need a few sharp answers — brand voice is a foundation that everything downstream (copy, campaigns, social posts) inherits, so a plausible-sounding guess here would be worse than a tailored one.

Three quick questions:

1. **Who exactly is your core audience?** The narrower the better — not "small businesses" but something like "solo consultants who hate looking corporate" or "Series A SaaS founders who want to sound credible but not stiff." Who are you really talking to?

2. **What's the one thing you want people to *feel* after reading anything from your brand?** (Examples: reassured, energized, like they're in on something, like they're talking to the smartest person in the room, like they found a trusted friend.) This is the emotional north star.

3. **Who or what are the alternatives — and how do you want to sound *different* from them?** This might be a competitor brand, an industry convention, or a generic tone (e.g., "we don't want to sound like corporate consulting firms that hide behind jargon"). Contrast is one of the fastest ways to define a voice.

Once I have these, I'll build you a full voice profile: 3–4 dimensions (each with a "we are / we're not" definition and do/don't examples), a tone guidance grid for key moments (sales, support, celebration, bad news), and a practical vocabulary guide your whole team can use.
