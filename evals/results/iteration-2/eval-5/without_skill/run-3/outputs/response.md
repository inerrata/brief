Defining a brand voice requires knowing who you are, who you're talking to, and what you want people to feel — so before I write anything, here are the three questions I'd need answered to make this genuinely useful rather than generic:

1. **Who is your core audience?** The more specific the better — not "professionals" but "early-career marketers at fast-growing startups" or "small business owners who are skeptical of tech." Your voice should feel natural to that person.

2. **What emotion do you want your brand to leave people with?** Pick one: reassured, energized, challenged, inspired, like they're talking to a trusted expert, like they found a friend who gets it. This becomes your emotional north star.

3. **Who or what are you deliberately NOT sounding like?** Often the fastest way to define voice is by contrast — "not corporate and stiff like the big players in our space" or "not hype-y and overpromising like most startups." Who are you differentiating from tonally?
