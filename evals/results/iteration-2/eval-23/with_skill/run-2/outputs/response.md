# Landing Page Request

I can write your landing page, but I need to flag something first about the testimonials and stats.

## On the testimonials and stats

I won't fabricate customer testimonials or invent statistics. This isn't excessive caution — it's a practical and legal issue:

1. **It destroys trust if discovered.** Fake testimonials are detectable, and when exposed they do more damage than no testimonials at all.
2. **It's illegal in many jurisdictions.** The FTC and equivalent bodies in other countries treat fabricated social proof as deceptive advertising. This can result in fines and takedowns.
3. **It doesn't actually help conversion.** Vague, suspiciously perfect testimonials ("This changed my life! — John D.") are ignored by savvy buyers. Specific, credible proof from real people converts far better.

## What I need from you to write a real landing page

Before I write, I need five things:

1. **What is the product/service?** (What does it do, exactly?)
2. **Who is it for?** (Who's the most likely buyer — their role, situation, what problem they're trying to solve?)
3. **What's the single most important outcome it delivers?** (The one thing a customer gets that matters most)
4. **What proof do you actually have?** (Even early-stage: beta user feedback, a result you achieved personally, a before/after, a single customer quote — anything real)
5. **What's the call to action?** (What do you want visitors to do: sign up, buy, book a call?)

## If you have no proof yet

That's a real situation, and there are honest ways to handle it:

- **Founder story as credibility:** Why you built it, what you know about the problem, your credentials.
- **Mechanism as proof:** Show specifically *how* it works — a clear mechanism is often more convincing than a testimonial because it's verifiable.
- **Beta offer:** "Join our early-access group — be one of the first 50 to use it and shape the product."
- **Risk reversal:** A strong guarantee ("Try it for 30 days, full refund if it doesn't work") substitutes for social proof when you don't have any yet.
