# What to Track for a New Paid Ads Channel

Here are the key metrics to set up from day one:

## Efficiency Metrics
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** — clicks ÷ impressions. Tells you if your ad creative and targeting are resonating.
- **CPC (Cost Per Click)** — what you pay per click. Benchmark varies by platform and industry.
- **CPM (Cost Per Thousand Impressions)** — useful for brand awareness campaigns.

## Conversion Metrics
- **Conversion Rate** — percentage of clicks that complete your goal action (purchase, signup, lead form).
- **Cost Per Conversion / CPL (Cost Per Lead)** — total spend ÷ number of conversions.
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — total spend ÷ new customers acquired. The true cost to get a customer.

## Revenue / Business Metrics
- **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** — revenue generated ÷ ad spend. A 3× ROAS means $3 back per $1 spent.
- **LTV:CAC ratio** — lifetime customer value vs. what you spent to acquire them. Aim for 3:1 or better.
- **CAC Payback Period** — how many months until you recoup acquisition cost.

## Attribution Setup
- Tag every ad URL with UTM parameters (source, medium, campaign, ad) so you can attribute conversions to the right ad.
- Decide on an attribution model (last-click is common but imperfect; position-based is often more accurate).

## What to Check on What Schedule
- **Weekly**: CTR, CPC, conversion rate, spend vs. budget
- **Monthly**: CAC, ROAS, LTV:CAC

Start by picking one primary goal metric (usually CAC or ROAS) and use the rest as diagnostic tools to explain why that number is moving.
