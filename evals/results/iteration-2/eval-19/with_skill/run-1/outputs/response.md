# What to Track for a New Paid Ads Channel

When you launch a new paid ads channel, track metrics across three layers: efficiency, funnel performance, and business impact. Here's what matters and why.

---

## Layer 1 — Efficiency (Is the channel working at all?)

- **Impressions & Reach** — how many people are seeing your ads. A baseline for everything else.
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** — clicks ÷ impressions. Low CTR means your creative or targeting is off; the ad isn't compelling enough to earn the click.
- **Cost Per Click (CPC)** — what you're paying for each click. Track alongside CTR; a high CPC with high CTR can still be efficient if downstream conversion is strong.
- **Cost Per Lead (CPL)** — if your goal is lead generation, this is the top efficiency metric.

## Layer 2 — Funnel Performance (Are clicks turning into customers?)

- **Conversion Rate** — conversions ÷ clicks. The single most important number after CTR. A high-traffic, low-conversion channel is expensive noise.
- **Cost Per Acquisition (CAC)** — total ad spend ÷ new customers acquired. This is the channel's true cost, not just CPC.
- **Landing Page Metrics** — bounce rate, time on page, and scroll depth tell you whether the traffic arriving is engaged or confused.

## Layer 3 — Business Impact (Is this channel profitable?)

- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)** — revenue from ads ÷ ad spend. A ROAS of 3× means every $1 spent returns $3 in revenue.
- **LTV:CAC Ratio** — lifetime value ÷ customer acquisition cost. A healthy benchmark is roughly 3:1 or better. Below 1:1 you're losing money per customer.
- **CAC Payback Period** — CAC ÷ monthly gross margin per customer. How many months until you recoup what you spent to acquire a customer.

---

## Practical setup for a new channel

**Pick one primary metric** tied to your goal — usually CAC or ROAS — and treat the rest as diagnostics. If your CAC is too high, work back through the funnel: is it a CTR problem (targeting/creative), a landing page problem (conversion rate), or a qualification problem (wrong audience converting at wrong rates)?

**Track attribution from day one.** Use UTM parameters on every ad link so you know which campaign, ad group, and creative is driving results. Without this, you're aggregating noise.

**Set a spend threshold before judging.** New channels need enough data to be meaningful — typically at least 30–50 conversion events before drawing conclusions. Calling a channel dead on 5 clicks is premature.

**Watch the leading indicators weekly, lagging indicators monthly:**
- Weekly: CTR, CPC, conversion rate, spend pacing
- Monthly: CAC, ROAS, LTV:CAC, CAC payback period

---

## Key formulas for reference

- **CAC** = total ad spend ÷ new customers acquired
- **ROAS** = revenue from ads ÷ ad spend
- **LTV:CAC** = customer lifetime value ÷ CAC
- **CAC Payback** = CAC ÷ monthly gross margin per customer

Start with these. Once the channel matures, layer in attribution modeling and incrementality testing to understand true contribution — but in early days, focus on whether CAC is sustainable and ROAS is positive.
