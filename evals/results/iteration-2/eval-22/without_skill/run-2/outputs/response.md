# How to Get Your First 100 Customers

Getting from zero to 100 customers requires going to find your customers — they won't stumble onto a new, unknown store. Here's how to do it in phases.

---

## Phase 1: Direct outreach (customers 1–20)

**Personal network:** Message friends, family, and contacts who fit your product. Ask them to buy, share, or give feedback. Be direct and personal — not a mass blast.

**Community outreach:** Find online communities where your target customer hangs out (subreddits, Facebook Groups, Discord servers, forums). Participate genuinely. When your product is relevant, share it.

**Seed with free product:** Give 5–10 units to people in exchange for honest reviews and testimonials. Real social proof converts better than anything else you can produce at this stage.

---

## Phase 2: Build organic presence (customers 20–60)

**Pick one social channel and commit.** Where does your buyer spend time?
- Visual products: Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest
- Discussion/community: Reddit, Twitter/X
- Tutorials/demos: YouTube, TikTok

Post 3–5x per week: product-in-use, behind-the-scenes, customer results, problem-solution content.

**Build your email list from day one.** Offer a small incentive (10% off, free guide) to capture emails. Email converts better than social over time — build this asset early.

**Micro-influencer gifting.** Find creators with 1k–20k followers in your niche. Offer your product for free in exchange for an honest post. Low cost, high targeting.

---

## Phase 3: Amplify with paid (customers 60–100+)

Don't run paid ads until you have proof your product sells organically and your page converts. Then:

1. **Retargeting first** — people who visited but didn't buy are the warmest possible audience.
2. **Boost your best organic content** — take the post that performed best and put small daily spend behind it.

---

## Key principle

At 0 customers, distribution is your job, not the algorithm's. Go find people — don't wait for them to find you.
