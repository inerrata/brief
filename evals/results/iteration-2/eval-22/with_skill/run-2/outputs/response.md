# How to Get Your First 100 Customers

Assuming you're running an e-commerce or DTC online store selling physical or digital products; adjust if your store type differs.

Getting from 0 to 100 customers is a different game than scaling. At this stage, you need traction, proof, and signal — not a full marketing machine. Here's the approach.

---

## Phase 1: Do things that don't scale (customers 1–20)

Your first customers will not come from SEO or ads. They come from direct, personal outreach.

**Your personal network first:**
- Message every relevant person in your network who might buy or share. Be direct: "I just launched [store]. Would you check it out / buy one / share it with someone who might love it?"
- Don't blanket-message everyone — target people who actually fit your product.

**Direct outreach in communities:**
- Find where your target customer already spends time: Reddit, Facebook Groups, Discord servers, forums, Instagram/TikTok niches.
- Participate genuinely, then share your store when relevant. "I actually built something for exactly this problem — here's the link" beats cold posting.
- DM people who post about the problem your product solves.

**Give a few away.**
Get 5–10 products in front of people in exchange for honest reviews and social proof. User-generated content from real people converts better than any ad you can run at this stage.

---

## Phase 2: Build a small but loyal audience (customers 20–60)

**Nail one social channel.**
Don't try to be everywhere. Pick one platform where your buyer lives and post consistently:
- Product-first visuals: Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest
- Community and conversation: Reddit, Twitter/X, Facebook Groups
- Education + demonstration: YouTube, TikTok

Post 3–5x per week. Mix content types: product-in-use, behind-the-scenes, customer results, problem/solution.

**Build an email list from day one.**
Every visitor who doesn't buy is a potential future buyer. Offer a small incentive (10% off, a free guide, early access) to capture emails. Email is the highest-converting channel you'll have — own it.

**Leverage micro-influencers.**
Find creators with 1k–20k followers in your niche. Offer product in exchange for an honest post. At this size, engagement is often higher than mega-influencers, and the cost is just your product.

---

## Phase 3: Add paid amplification once you have signal (customers 60–100+)

**Don't run paid ads without signal.**
Before spending money on ads, you need:
- A proven product (people are buying and liking it)
- A converting landing page (organic traffic converts at >1%)
- A winning creative concept (from your organic content — use what worked)

**Start with retargeting.**
People who visited your store but didn't buy are the warmest audience. A retargeting ad on Meta/Instagram reminding them of what they looked at is often the highest-ROAS ad you can run.

**Promote your best organic content.**
Take the social post that got the most engagement organically and put $20–50/day behind it. You already know it resonates.

---

## What to track

- Traffic sources (where are visitors coming from?)
- Conversion rate (are visitors buying?)
- Cost per order (if using paid)
- Where your first 30 customers came from — this tells you what to double down on

---

## The real unlock

Most online stores fail to get first customers not because the product is bad, but because they wait for customers to find them. You have to go find your customers first. Direct outreach, community presence, and genuine social content put you in motion — paid ads amplify motion you've already created.
