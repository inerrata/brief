Assuming this is a SaaS or subscription product; adjust copy, timing, and incentive to match your product, pricing, and brand voice.

# Win-Back Sequence — Customers Inactive for 60+ Days

**Trigger:** No login for 60 consecutive days.
**Goal:** Reactivate the customer. If they don't re-engage after the full sequence, suppress them from marketing sends to protect deliverability and list quality.

---

## Email 1 — The Check-In (Day 60 of inactivity)

**Subject:** Haven't seen you in a while, [First name]
**Preview text:** Is everything okay with your account?

---

Hey [First name],

It's been a couple of months since you last logged into [Product name], and I wanted to check in.

Is there something that got in the way, or something that wasn't working for you? I'd genuinely like to know — hit reply and tell me.

If you just got busy (it happens), here's a direct link back in: **[Log in now →]**

[Your name / Support team]

---

**Why this works:** Short, human, no pressure. Acknowledges the gap honestly. The question invites a reply, which gives you useful signal and improves deliverability.

---

## Email 2 — What's New (Day 65)

**Subject:** Here's what changed since you last logged in
**Preview text:** A few things worth knowing about.

---

Hey [First name],

In case you've been away — here's what's new in [Product name] since you last logged in:

- **[New feature or improvement]** — [one sentence on the benefit it delivers]
- **[New feature or improvement]** — [one sentence on the benefit it delivers]
- **[New feature or improvement]** — [one sentence on the benefit it delivers]

[If your product has usage-based value:] Customers who've been using [specific feature] are seeing [specific result — e.g., "saving an average of 3 hours per week on X"].

Worth a look? **[Log back in →]**

[Your name]

---

**Why this works:** Gives a concrete reason to return — things are different now. Use real updates, not filler. If nothing meaningful has changed, skip this email or replace it with a best-practices resource that reignites interest.

---

## Email 3 — The Offer (Day 72)

**Subject:** A reason to come back, [First name]
**Preview text:** Something for you if you're ready to jump back in.

---

Hey [First name],

I know life gets busy. If you've been meaning to get back to [Product name] but haven't found the time — here's a push.

For the next 7 days, we're offering [active customers who've been away]: **[specific incentive — e.g., one free month, a complimentary onboarding call, 20% off next renewal, access to a premium feature]**.

Use the link below to log in and claim it. No hoops.

**[Come back and claim your [offer] →]**

Offer expires [date — 7 days from send].

[Your name]

---

**Notes on the offer:** Use an incentive proportional to your product's value and your customer acquisition cost. A free month or a complimentary session is often more effective than a discount because it demonstrates value rather than just reducing price. Only use real scarcity — a made-up deadline burns trust.

---

## Email 4 — Final Notice (Day 79)

**Subject:** Last note from us, [First name]
**Preview text:** We'll stop reaching out after this.

---

Hey [First name],

This is the last email I'll send about coming back to [Product name].

If it's not a fit right now, that's completely okay. You can [cancel / pause / keep your account at no cost — whatever applies].

If there's something specific that stopped you from using it — a missing feature, a confusing part, a price issue — I'd genuinely appreciate knowing. Reply to this email and I'll read it.

If you do want to jump back in: **[Log in →]**

Either way, thanks for trying [Product name]. I hope we'll see you again.

[Your name]

---

**Why this works:** Honest and respectful closing. "Last note" language drives action from fence-sitters. Offering a clean exit (cancel/pause) is good customer service and protects your NPS and review profile. Asking for feedback gives you product intel.

---

## Post-sequence action

- **No response after Email 4:** Remove from active marketing sends. Keep in a suppressed segment for potential future win-back if major product changes happen.
- **Opened but didn't re-engage:** Flag for a targeted offer in 30–60 days if product updates make it relevant.
- **Replied with feedback:** Route to customer success or founder — this is gold. These responses often reveal fixable friction that's silently killing retention.

---

## Sequence summary

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Check-in | Day 60 | Acknowledge gap, invite reply, low-friction return |
| 2 — What's new | Day 65 | Give a reason to come back (new value) |
| 3 — The offer | Day 72 | Incentive to re-engage with urgency |
| 4 — Final notice | Day 79 | Last chance + honest close |
