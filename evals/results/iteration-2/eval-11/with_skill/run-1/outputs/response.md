An ICP is a strategy foundation — everything from your messaging to your channel choices inherits from it, so I want to get it right rather than guess and produce something that looks authoritative but is built on the wrong assumptions.

Three quick questions before I build it:

1. **Who are your best current customers?** If you have paying customers already, who are the ones who get the most value, complain the least, and are most likely to refer others? (Role, company size, industry — even "freelancers" vs. "small agencies" vs. "small businesses" makes a big difference.)

2. **What problem does your invoicing tool solve that others don't?** Is it speed, simplicity, integrations with accounting tools, support for recurring billing, international/multi-currency invoicing, something else? What's the one thing that makes a customer choose you over FreshBooks, Wave, or just a Word template?

3. **Who are you NOT a fit for?** Understanding who you've lost deals to, or who churns, sharpens the ICP as much as knowing who you win.

Once I have these, I'll build a full ICP covering: firmographics, the specific buyer profile, their goals and pains, what triggers them to look for a solution, what they compare you against, and the messaging implications.
