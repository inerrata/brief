## 2. Search Intent
**Type:** Commercial investigation — the searcher is comparing options before making a purchase or implementation decision.

**What the searcher wants:** A clear, trustworthy list of CRM options built for or suitable for nonprofits, with enough differentiation to make a choice. They are NOT looking for a generic CRM explanation — they already know they need a CRM; they want to know which one fits a nonprofit's specific needs (donor tracking, volunteer management, grant reporting, budget constraints).

**Content type that matches:** Listicle/comparison article — "Best CRM for Nonprofits in 2025: [X] Top Options Compared." This is what the top results will almost certainly be; confirm by searching the keyword before writing.

## 8. Differentiation Angle

To rank above existing comparison articles, this piece needs one of the following:
- **Original data:** Survey nonprofit operators on their CRM usage/satisfaction; even 20–50 responses creates a citable stat.
- **Deeper specificity:** Most comparison articles are generic. This piece should speak directly to nonprofit-specific concerns: grant reporting workflows, donor retention metrics, board reporting templates, 501(c)(3) discount programs.
- **Honest verdict:** Don't hedge on every recommendation. Tell the reader clearly: "If you're a small nonprofit with under 500 donors and a limited budget, start with Little Green Light or HubSpot free. If you have over 5,000 donors and need grant management, look at Bloomerang or Salesforce NPSP."

## 11. E-E-A-T Signals to Include

- **Author expertise:** Attribute to someone with nonprofit tech or CRM implementation experience. Include a short bio.
- **First-hand testing:** Note whether the author or team has tested/used the tools reviewed. Even "we signed up for free trials of each tool" is meaningful.
- **Last updated date:** Prominently displayed — CRM pricing and features change; readers need to trust this is current.
- **Cite sources:** Link to each CRM's official pricing page; cite nonprofit discount program details with sources.
- **Transparency on methodology:** Brief note on how tools were selected/evaluated.
