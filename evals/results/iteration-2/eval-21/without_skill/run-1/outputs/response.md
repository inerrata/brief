# Your Launch Plan: Notion Template Business, No Audience, One Month Out

You have one month, a paid product, and zero audience. Here's a realistic plan that can actually work in that window.

---

## The core challenge

No audience means you can't just "post and hope." You need to put yourself in front of people who already want what you're building — before you try to sell anything.

---

## Week 1–2: Find your buyers and earn their attention

**Step 1: Define who your template is for, specifically.**
"Notion users" is not a target audience. "Freelancers who want to track clients and invoices in Notion" or "students building a personal knowledge management system" is. The more specific, the easier everything else gets.

**Step 2: Go where they already are.**
- Reddit: r/Notion, r/productivity, plus subreddits specific to your niche (r/freelance, r/studytips, etc.)
- Twitter/X: search Notion-related hashtags and accounts
- Facebook Groups for Notion, productivity, or your niche
- Notion's own template gallery and community
- YouTube comments on popular Notion tutorial videos

**Step 3: Show up and be helpful — don't sell yet.**
Answer questions. Share a free tip or a mini-template. Demonstrate that you know the problem. This builds recognition and trust, which you'll cash in at launch.

**Step 4: Start a waitlist now.**
Set up a free Gumroad or Lemon Squeezy page, or a simple Carrd landing page. "I'm building [X template that does Y] — join the list for early access and a launch discount." Even 30 signups is something.

---

## Week 2–3: Build proof before you need it

Proof is the difference between "I'll buy this" and "maybe later."

- Give your template to 3–5 people in exchange for feedback and a short testimonial.
- Get specific results if you can: "I used this to cut my weekly review time in half" beats "it's great!"
- Record a quick Loom walkthrough of the template in use — real demonstration beats any description.

---

## Week 3–4: Launch

**Pre-launch (3–5 days out):**
- Email your waitlist with a heads-up and the promise of a launch discount (e.g., 30% off, first 48 hours only).
- Tease the launch in your communities.

**Launch day:**
- Post simultaneously in all the communities you've been active in. Lead with the problem it solves, show the template, include a testimonial or screenshot.
- Email your list: "it's live, here's your discount."
- Submit to Notion template directories — several curated galleries exist and can drive traffic.
- Be present and responsive all day.

**Post-launch:**
- Post again a few days later with early buyer reactions or a use-case story.
- Follow up with your list once more before the discount expires.

---

## Key principle

One month is short. Don't spread thin across five platforms. Pick the one place your buyer hangs out most and go deep there. Real presence in one community will outperform shallow presence in five.
