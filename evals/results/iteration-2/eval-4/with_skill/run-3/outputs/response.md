A positioning statement is load-bearing — everything downstream (copy, campaigns, sales pitch) inherits its logic. Writing one on guessed inputs produces something that looks authoritative but fits no one specifically. So before I write this, three quick things:

1. **Narrowest accurate description of the target customer?** For example: "creative agencies of 5–15 people managing 3–10 active client projects," or "growth agencies running paid and SEO campaigns for mid-market clients." The more specific, the more the positioning can say something true.

2. **The one thing you do that the main alternatives don't?** Not a feature list — the dimension where you genuinely win and where agencies actually make their buying decision. Is it built around client deliverables and retainer billing? Does it give clients visibility without exposing your internal process? Does it handle scope creep better than anything else?

3. **What's the real alternative you're replacing?** A specific tool (ClickUp, Monday, Teamwork), a mix of spreadsheets and Slack, or just how agencies have always stumbled through project delivery?

Answer those three and I'll write the positioning statement straight away.
