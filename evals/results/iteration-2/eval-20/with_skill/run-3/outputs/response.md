# Is a $400 CAC Good at $50/month with 18-Month Retention?

Short answer: yes, this is a healthy unit economics profile. Here's the math.

---

## The numbers

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** = $50/month × 18 months = **$900**

**CAC** = **$400**

**LTV:CAC ratio** = $900 ÷ $400 = **2.25:1**

**CAC Payback Period** = $400 ÷ $50/month = **8 months**

---

## How to read these

**LTV:CAC of 2.25:1** — A common rule of thumb benchmark is roughly 3:1 for a healthy SaaS or subscription business. At 2.25:1 you're profitable per customer, but you have less margin buffer than the benchmark suggests is ideal. You're not losing money, but there's room to either grow LTV (reduce churn, upsell) or reduce CAC (improve conversion efficiency).

**Payback period of 8 months** — This means you recoup your acquisition cost in about 8 months. Whether that's good depends on your cash position:
- If you're bootstrapped or cash-constrained, 8 months is a meaningful wait — growth can strain cash flow.
- If you have runway or investors, 8 months is quite reasonable for a subscription business.

---

## One caveat: gross margin matters

The formulas above use revenue, not margin. If your gross margin is, say, 70%, the margin-adjusted LTV is $630 (not $900), and your LTV:CAC drops to 1.58:1 — which is tighter.

For a cleaner picture:
- **Margin-adjusted LTV** = $50 × gross margin % × 18 months
- **Margin-based CAC payback** = $400 ÷ ($50 × gross margin %)

If you're a software business with ~80% margins, you're still comfortably profitable per customer. If you have significant cost of goods (physical product, service delivery), recalculate with your actual margin.

---

## Bottom line

Your unit economics work. The LTV:CAC is positive and the payback period is manageable. The main levers to improve the picture:
1. **Reduce churn** (extend average life beyond 18 months — even 2 more months adds $100 to LTV)
2. **Add upsell/expansion revenue** (increases LTV without touching CAC)
3. **Improve conversion efficiency** (lower CAC through better targeting or landing page optimization)

A $400 CAC at these retention numbers is not a red flag — it's a reasonable foundation to build on.
