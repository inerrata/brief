Assuming this is for a general content/knowledge newsletter; adjust tone, timing, and specifics to fit your brand and audience.

---

# Welcome Sequence — 5 Emails

## Email 1 — Welcome (Send immediately on subscribe)

**Subject:** You're in — here's what to expect
**Preview text:** Plus a quick ask that'll make everything I send more useful.

Hey [First name],

Welcome — really glad you're here.

Here's what you signed up for: [brief description of what the newsletter covers and how often you send].

One quick favor: hit reply and tell me the #1 thing you're trying to figure out right now when it comes to [your topic]. I read every reply, and your answer helps me write things that actually matter to you.

Talk soon,
[Your name]

---

## Email 2 — Your Story (Send day 2)

**Subject:** Why I write this newsletter
**Preview text:** It started with a problem I couldn't find a good answer to.

Hey [First name],

Before I send you any more useful stuff, I want you to know who's writing these emails.

[2–3 short paragraphs: the experience or struggle that led you to this topic, what you learned, why you now write about it. Be specific. Readers connect to real stories, not polished bios.]

I started this newsletter because I wanted to write the resource I wished had existed when I was figuring this out.

That's what I'll keep doing.

[Your name]

---

## Email 3 — Best Resource (Send day 4)

**Subject:** The most useful thing I've made on [topic]
**Preview text:** Worth 10 minutes of your time.

Hey [First name],

Short email today — I want to point you to the single most useful thing I've created.

[Describe your best piece of content, guide, template, or resource. Say specifically what it helps the reader do or understand.]

[Link here]

This is the foundation for a lot of what I write. Once you've read it, everything else I send will land better.

[Your name]

---

## Email 4 — Social Proof (Send day 6)

**Subject:** What readers say
**Preview text:** Results from people dealing with the same thing you are.

Hey [First name],

I'd rather show you the newsletter is worth your time than just tell you.

Here's what some readers have shared:

> "[Specific testimonial — ideally with a concrete result or specific observation]"
> — [Name, context]

> "[Second testimonial — different angle or reader type]"
> — [Name, context]

[If you're just starting and don't have testimonials yet: share a specific result you've helped someone achieve, a relevant data point, or what a reader recently replied to you.]

[Your name]

---

## Email 5 — Soft Offer (Send day 8)

**Subject:** One more thing, when you're ready
**Preview text:** No pressure. Just want you to know it exists.

Hey [First name],

You've had a week of emails from me. I hope at least a few of them were worth your time.

The newsletter will always be free — that's not changing.

But if you want to go deeper, I have [product/service/community]: [one-sentence description of what it is and who it's for].

[Link: Learn more]

No urgency. I just want you to know it's there when the time is right.

Either way, I'll see you in your inbox [next send day].

[Your name]

---

## Timing and tips

- **Default pacing:** Day 0 (immediate), Day 2, Day 4, Day 6, Day 8. Adjust based on your audience's pace.
- **After email 5:** Shift subscribers to your regular newsletter cadence.
- **Reply encouragement:** Ask for replies in emails 1 and 3 especially — inbox placement improves when subscribers reply to you.
- **Tone:** Be a person, not a brand. Newsletters live and die on voice.
