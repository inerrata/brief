# Welcome Sequence — 5-Email Flow

Triggered immediately on subscribe. The goal: turn a new subscriber into an engaged, trusting reader who's primed to eventually buy or take the next step with you.

---

## Email 1 — Welcome + Delivery (Send immediately)

**Subject:** You're in — here's what happens next
**Preview text:** Plus the one thing I want you to do right now.

---

Hey [First name],

Welcome — glad you're here.

[If you offered a lead magnet: Here's the [guide/checklist/resource] you signed up for: [LINK]. ]

Here's what you can expect from me:

- **[Cadence]** — I send [weekly/biweekly] on [day]
- **[Topic focus]** — Every issue covers [main topic: e.g., "practical strategies for leading remote teams"]
- **No fluff** — I write the things I actually wish I'd known

One thing I'd love: hit reply and tell me the single biggest challenge you're dealing with right now related to [topic]. I read every reply, and your answer shapes what I write next.

Talk soon,
[Name]

---

## Email 2 — Your Story / Why (Send day 2)

**Subject:** Why I started this newsletter
**Preview text:** The short version: I got it wrong for years.

---

Hey [First name],

Before I send you more useful stuff, I want to tell you why I started this.

[2–3 short paragraphs: your origin story, the problem you personally ran into, what you learned, why you now write about it. Be specific and human — this is not a resume. Readers connect to struggle, not credentials.]

The reason I share this: I want you to know who's behind these emails and why I care about [topic]. Everything I write is based on [experience/research/practice] — not recycled advice.

If that sounds like what you're looking for, we're going to get along just fine.

[Name]

---

## Email 3 — Best Value (Send day 4)

**Subject:** The most useful thing I've written on [topic]
**Preview text:** Most people skip this. Don't.

---

Hey [First name],

Quick one today — I want to give you the single most valuable thing I've produced.

[Introduce your best resource: a guide, a framework, a past issue, a free tool. Describe specifically what it does for the reader and why it matters.]

[LINK: Read/get it here]

[1–2 sentences on what to do with it or what insight to take from it.]

This is the foundation I build everything else on. Once you've read it, the rest of what I send will make more sense.

[Name]

---

## Email 4 — Social Proof (Send day 6)

**Subject:** What [readers/clients/members] say about [newsletter name or your work]
**Preview text:** Results from people in your exact situation.

---

Hey [First name],

I don't want to just tell you this newsletter is worth your time — I want to show you.

Here's what some readers have said:

> "[Specific testimonial with a concrete result or observation — e.g., 'I used the framework from issue #12 and cut my team's onboarding time by 40%.']"
> — [Name, Role, Company]

> "[Second testimonial — different angle, ideally from a different type of reader.]"
> — [Name, Role]

[If you don't have testimonials yet: share a specific outcome you've helped someone achieve, or a data point from your own experience — something that makes the value concrete.]

The people getting the most from this newsletter are [describe ideal reader]. If that's you, you're in the right place.

[Name]

---

## Email 5 — Soft Offer (Send day 8)

**Subject:** When you're ready for the next step
**Preview text:** No pressure — but here's what I have for you.

---

Hey [First name],

You've been on the list for about a week. I hope the emails so far have been worth your time.

For most readers, the newsletter is enough — and I'll keep sending it for free, forever.

But if you want to go deeper, here's what I offer:

**[Your product/service/community in one sentence — e.g., "A 6-week group program for people managers navigating their first remote team."]**

[2–3 bullet points of specific outcomes/what's included]

[LINK: Learn more / See if it's a fit]

No urgency, no pressure. I just want you to know it exists when the time is right.

Either way, I'll be in your inbox [next send day] with [brief tease of next issue's value].

[Name]

---

## Notes on implementation

- **Spacing:** The 8-day sequence above is a reasonable default. Tighten to 5 days if your audience is fast-moving; extend to 2 weeks if they're slower to engage (e.g., senior executives).
- **Personalization:** Even using first name throughout makes a measurable difference in reply rate.
- **Deliverability:** Make sure SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are configured on your sending domain. Start sends to the most engaged segments first if your list is large.
- **After email 5:** Move subscribers into your regular newsletter cadence.
