A content calendar for a B2B fintech startup is only as useful as the strategy it sits on — a generic one wastes your team's time. Before I build it, three quick questions so the calendar is actually grounded:

1. **Who is your primary audience, and what stage are they in?** For example: CFOs at mid-market companies evaluating a new payments tool, or finance ops managers at Series A startups who are just becoming aware that their current process is broken. The persona and their awareness level determines what types of content to lead with.

2. **What's your primary business goal for content in the next 90 days?** Options: build brand awareness and reach, generate inbound leads, support sales cycles (i.e., nurture known prospects), or retain and expand existing customers. A content calendar that tries to do all four at once usually does none well.

3. **What channels and resources do you actually have?** For instance: one content writer posting twice a week on LinkedIn + a monthly blog, or a full team running LinkedIn, email, and a podcast. Cadence and format have to match what you can sustain — an unsustainable plan is worse than a modest one.

Once I have these, I'll build you a full 4-week calendar with: content pillars, funnel-stage mapping (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU), specific working titles, formats, channels, CTAs, and a recommended 60/25/15 content mix (educational/social proof/promotional).
