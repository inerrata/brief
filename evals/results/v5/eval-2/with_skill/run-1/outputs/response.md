A tagline like "Crush your goals" almost never survives a literal translation — its power is in the punchy, slightly aggressive idiom. *Ziele zerquetschen* ("squash your goals") sounds violent and odd in German. So this is a **transcreation** job: recreate the *feeling* (confident, energetic, you-can-dominate-this), not the words.

Below are options that take genuinely different angles. I don't know your audience, channel, or brand voice, so I've assumed a **broad consumer/fitness-or-productivity vibe with an informal "du" register** — the most common fit for "Crush your goals." Adjust if your brand is more formal (Sie) or B2B.

## Recommended options

**1. "Erreich deine Ziele. Mit Wucht."** — *Reach your goals. With force.*
Closest to the original's punch. "Mit Wucht" (with force/impact) carries the "crush" energy without the violent literal image. Clean, confident, very German-natural.

**2. "Ran an deine Ziele."** — *Go get your goals / Get after your goals.*
Short, idiomatic, high-energy call to action. "Ran an..." is a native motivational phrase that travels well in fitness, sports, and hustle contexts. Most slogan-like and memorable.

**3. "Mach deine Ziele platt."** — *Flatten your goals.*
The boldest, closest in spirit to "crush." Informal and punchy, with the same playful aggression. Best if your brand voice is young, gym/sports, and irreverent — riskier for a buttoned-up B2B audience.

## Other angles worth a look
- **"Zielen. Treffen. Weiter."** — *Aim. Hit. Next.* — staccato, momentum-driven, very modern.
- **"Deine Ziele haben keine Chance."** — *Your goals don't stand a chance.* — confident, slightly cheeky, keeps the "dominate" framing.
- **Formal/Sie version** (if needed): **"Erreichen Sie Ihre Ziele. Mit Wucht."**

## My pick
If you want one safe, high-energy, broadly usable line: **"Ran an deine Ziele."** If you want to keep the swagger of "crush": **"Mach deine Ziele platt."**

## Two things before you ship
1. **Get a native, in-market reviewer to sign off.** Machine-style translation is only a draft for a tagline — register, regional feel, and any unintended connotation need a real German native eye (ideally in your target country; tone can differ between Germany, Austria, and Switzerland).
2. **Confirm the register.** "Du" (informal) suits consumer/fitness/lifestyle. If your brand or audience is corporate, switch to "Sie" — it changes the whole feel.
