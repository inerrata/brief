# German translation: "Crush your goals"

A tagline like this rarely translates word-for-word. The English plays on "crush" = to dominate/smash, which sounds energetic and slightly aggressive in a motivating way. German has several ways to capture that, depending on the tone and audience you want. Here are options, from most natural to most literal.

## Recommended

**"Zerschmettere deine Ziele"**
Literal-ish but punchy. "Zerschmettern" = to smash/shatter, carrying the same forceful, athletic energy as "crush." This is the closest match in both meaning and attitude, and it reads well as a campaign line.

## Strong alternatives

| German | Tone / nuance | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **"Erreiche deine Ziele — mit voller Power"** | Achieve your goals, full power | Broad audience, less aggressive |
| **"Übertriff deine Ziele"** | *Surpass / exceed* your goals | Aspirational, sounds confident and positive |
| **"Pulverisiere deine Ziele"** | *Pulverize* your goals | Very high-energy, fitness/gaming vibe |
| **"Mach deine Ziele platt"** | Colloquial "flatten them" | Casual, youthful, informal |
| **"Knack deine Ziele"** | *Crack* your goals (like cracking a tough problem) | Snappy, short, friendly |

## Formality note (important for German)

German distinguishes informal **du** from formal **Sie**. The options above use the informal **du** ("deine"), which fits most modern, motivational consumer marketing. If your brand addresses customers formally, switch to **Sie**:

- **"Zerschmettern Sie Ihre Ziele"**
- **"Übertreffen Sie Ihre Ziele"**

## A couple of questions to lock in the best choice

1. **Tone:** Do you want to keep the aggressive "crush" energy, or lean more toward positive/aspirational ("surpass/achieve")?
2. **Audience & address:** Informal (**du**) or formal (**Sie**)? And is this consumer-facing (fitness, lifestyle) or B2B?
3. **Context:** Is "goals" literal life/business goals, or sports/fitness goals? That affects which verb lands best.

If you don't have a preference, my pick is **"Zerschmettere deine Ziele"** for a direct campaign translation, or **"Übertriff deine Ziele"** if you want something more polished and positive.
