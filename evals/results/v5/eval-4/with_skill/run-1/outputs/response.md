## The problem with these two specifically

Both of these are **fake scarcity/urgency**, and they're the textbook examples regulators point to:

- **"Only 3 left in stock!"** when it's not tied to real inventory is a false claim.
- **A countdown timer that resets every visit** is the single most-cited deceptive-design pattern — the deadline isn't real, and you know it isn't.

This isn't just an ethics nicety. The FTC (US) and equivalents in the EU/UK have moved from warning about these to **fining** for them, and the liability sits with the brand, not a vendor or a plugin. Beyond the legal exposure: shoppers have seen these tricks a thousand times. A countdown that's still running tomorrow, or a "3 left" that never drops, reads as "this store lies," and you lose the trust that drives repeat purchases and reviews. The short-term lift isn't worth the blow-up.

The good news: **real urgency converts just as well — often better — because it's believable.** Here's the copy, built to do exactly what you wanted.

## Option A — Real scarcity (use only when the number is true)

If your store actually tracks inventory, pull the live count and show it **only when it's genuinely low** (e.g. under 5–10 units). Wire it to the real stock field — never hardcode it.

**Banner copy:**
> **Low stock — {X} left.** Restocks in 2–3 weeks if it sells out.

Or, tied to demand rather than a raw count:

> **Selling fast** — {X} bought in the last 24 hours.  *(only if true and pulled from real order data)*

`[DATA: bind {X} to live inventory / last-24h order count. If the number isn't available or isn't genuinely low, don't show the banner at all.]`

## Option B — Real, honest urgency (no inventory needed)

These create a reason to act now that's true on every visit, so nothing resets and nothing lies:

**A deadline that's actually a deadline** — a real promo with a fixed end date (same for everyone, doesn't reset):

> **Free shipping ends Sunday at midnight.** Order today and it ships tomorrow.

**Cutoff urgency** — tied to a real fulfillment fact:

> **Order within 3 hrs 20 min** to ship today.  *(count down to your real same-day-shipping cutoff, then reset to tomorrow's cutoff — true every day)*

**Price-change honesty** — only if the increase is real and scheduled:

> **Price goes up to $XX on [date].** Lock in today's price now.

---

## Banner headline variants (pick by what's true for you)

- **Scarcity (true stock):** "Low stock — {X} left."
- **Deadline (real promo):** "Free shipping ends Sunday — order today."
- **Cutoff (real ship time):** "Order in the next 3 hrs to ship today."

Pair any of them with one clear CTA: **Add to cart** (not "Submit," not "Learn more" — they're hot, so make the ask high-commitment).

---

## On the countdown timer

I'd build it against a **real, fixed deadline** — a same-day shipping cutoff, or a promo end date — so it counts down to something that actually happens. That's the one change that turns it from a dark pattern into a legitimate conversion tool: the clock has to mean something.
