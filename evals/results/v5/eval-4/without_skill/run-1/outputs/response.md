# Urgency Copy — With One Important Flag First

I can absolutely write punchy urgency copy for you. Before I hand it over, I want to be straight about one mechanic in the brief, because it affects both your legal exposure and your conversion rate over time.

## The one thing I'd change

**A countdown timer that resets on every visit, and a "Only 3 left in stock!" banner that isn't tied to real inventory, are fake-urgency tactics.** If the timer restarts whenever someone reloads or comes back, and the "3 left" number doesn't reflect actual stock, the claim is false.

That matters for three concrete reasons:

1. **It's a legal risk.** In the US, the FTC has explicitly targeted deceptive countdown timers and false scarcity ("urgency and scarcity") claims as illegal dark patterns, with real fines. The UK's CMA and the EU's consumer-protection rules treat invented countdowns and fake "low stock" messages the same way. Many ecommerce platforms (and ad networks like Google) can also penalize or suspend you for it.
2. **It burns repeat customers and reputation.** Shoppers increasingly recognize the reset-on-refresh trick. The moment someone reloads and sees the timer jump back to 10:00, or buys an item that was "almost gone" weeks ago, trust drops — and trust is what actually drives repeat purchases and word of mouth.
3. **You don't need to fake it.** Real, honest urgency converts well and carries none of the above risk. Below I've written copy for both: honest mechanics I'd recommend, and the literal version you asked for so you can decide.

## Recommended: honest urgency that still converts

These are tied to things that are actually true, so you get the lift without the liability.

**Real low-stock banner (only show it when stock is genuinely low):**
- "Almost gone — only 3 left in stock."
- "Low stock alert: 3 remaining. Order soon to avoid the wait."
- "Selling fast — fewer than 5 left at this price."

**Real deadline timer (a genuine sale or shipping cutoff, same end time for everyone):**
- "Sale ends Sunday at midnight — 23:14:06 left."
- "Order in the next **02:41:18** for free next-day delivery."
- "Launch price ends Friday. Lock it in before the timer hits zero."

**Demand-based social proof (true and persuasive):**
- "12 people are viewing this right now."
- "Bought 40+ times this week."
- "Back in stock — and going quickly."

A shipping-cutoff timer ("order in the next X hours for delivery by [date]") is the strongest honest option I know: it's real, it resets legitimately every day, and it answers a question the buyer actually has.

## The literal version you asked for

If you still want the exact mechanic from the brief, here's tight copy for it. My strong recommendation is to make at least the timer a real, fixed deadline rather than a per-visit reset.

**Stock banner:**
- "Only 3 left in stock — don't miss out!"
- "Hurry — just 3 left!"

**Countdown timer:**
- "Offer ends in 09:59 — claim your discount now."
- "Your deal is reserved for 10:00. Checkout before time runs out."
- Button: "Grab it before it's gone"

## My recommendation in one line

Keep the *feel* of urgency, drop the *fakery*: show "Only 3 left" only when it's truly 3 left, and replace the per-visit reset with a real, shared deadline or a daily shipping cutoff. You'll get the conversion lift, keep your customers' trust, and stay clear of FTC/CMA dark-pattern rules.
