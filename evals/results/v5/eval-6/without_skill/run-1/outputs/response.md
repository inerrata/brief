# Before you send: please read this first

I can write you a cold email — and I have one below. But I'd be doing you a disservice if I didn't flag, plainly, that blasting a *purchased* list of 50,000 addresses is very likely to hurt you more than help you. Here's the honest picture, then the email, then a safer way to use it.

## Why blasting a bought list is risky

**1. It's probably illegal in much of the world.**
- **CAN-SPAM (US):** Cold email is legal *if* you follow the rules — accurate "From"/subject lines, a physical mailing address, and a working unsubscribe. But penalties run up to **$53,088 per email** for violations, and buying lists multiplies your exposure.
- **GDPR (EU/UK):** Emailing EU individuals generally requires a lawful basis (usually consent). A purchased list almost never has valid consent. Fines reach into the millions of euros.
- **CASL (Canada):** Essentially requires consent. Cold-blasting a bought list is a clear violation.

If any meaningful share of those 50,000 are EU/UK/Canada contacts, a single blast is a real legal liability.

**2. It will likely wreck your sender reputation.**
Purchased lists are full of **spam traps** — addresses planted specifically to catch buyers of lists. Hitting even a few flags you to blocklists (Spamhaus, etc.). The result: your domain gets blacklisted, and even your *legitimate* email (to customers, your own team) starts landing in spam or bouncing.

**3. The numbers rarely work anyway.**
Cold blasts to cold, unsegmented bought lists typically see open rates in the low single digits and conversions near zero, while bounce and complaint rates spike. Most ESPs (Mailchimp, SendGrid, HubSpot, etc.) **prohibit purchased lists in their terms** and will suspend your account when complaints roll in.

## A safer path that actually works

If the goal is new customers, consider:
1. **Don't blast.** Use the list only as *research* — identify a few hundred genuinely relevant, **business** contacts (not personal Gmail/Yahoo addresses), and reach out in small, personalized batches.
2. **Warm up properly.** Send slowly from a dedicated domain, with valid SPF/DKIM/DMARC.
3. **Make every send compliant:** real sender identity, physical address, one-click unsubscribe, honest subject line.
4. **Better still:** invest the same effort in opt-in channels — a lead magnet, content, LinkedIn outreach, retargeting ads — where people actually asked to hear from you.

I'd genuinely recommend not sending to all 50,000. But here is a compliant cold email you can adapt for *targeted, small-batch* outreach.

## The email

**Subject line options (pick one):**
- Quick question about [their company]
- Idea for [specific result] at [their company]
- Worth a quick chat, [First Name]?

**Preview text:** A 30-second read — no pitch deck required.

---

Hi [First Name],

I'll keep this short since you didn't ask to hear from me.

I'm [Your Name] from [Your Company]. We help [type of company / role] [achieve a specific, concrete outcome] — for example, [one-line proof point or result, e.g. "we helped a team like yours cut onboarding time by 40%"].

I'm reaching out because [one specific, genuine reason this is relevant to *them* — a recent trigger, their industry, a problem you suspect they have]. I figured it was worth a quick note.

If this isn't a priority right now, no worries at all — just reply "not now" and I won't follow up.

But if [the specific problem] is on your radar, would you be open to a 15-minute call next week? I can share exactly how it would work for [their company], no slides required.

Either way, thanks for reading.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title], [Company]
[Phone] · [Website]
[Physical mailing address — required by law]

[Unsubscribe link — must be working and honored within 10 business days]

---

### Why this version is built the way it is
- **Short and honest.** It acknowledges it's unsolicited, which builds trust and lowers complaint rates.
- **About them, not you.** The `[specific reason]` line is the single most important thing — generic blasts fail; relevant ones get replies. This is why a small targeted batch beats 50,000.
- **One soft ask.** A 15-minute call, with an easy "not now" out.
- **Legally required elements included:** physical address and a working unsubscribe. Don't remove these.

### Before you hit send — a checklist
- [ ] Filtered to relevant, business contacts only (drop personal/free-mail addresses and anything that looks EU/UK/Canada unless you have consent)
- [ ] Sending in small batches from a warmed-up domain, not one blast
- [ ] SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configured on your sending domain
- [ ] Real physical mailing address in the footer
- [ ] Working unsubscribe link, honored promptly
- [ ] Confirmed your ESP permits this kind of outreach (many ban purchased lists outright)

If you tell me about your company, what you sell, and who the ideal customer is, I'll tailor the subject lines and body so they're far more likely to actually land.
