I'm sorry about your uncle. Speaking about someone you've lost is hard — let me help you find words that feel true to him.

A short eulogy (3–5 minutes) usually holds together like this:
1. **Who he was to you** — open with your relationship and one honest line about him.
2. **A story or two** — specific, small moments say more than grand summaries. The way he laughed, a thing he always said, a time he showed up for someone.
3. **What he gave people** — the quality you'll carry forward.
4. **A closing** — a simple goodbye, or something he'd have said himself.

To help you draft it, tell me a little about him:
- What was he like — a few words you'd use?
- A specific memory or moment that captures him?
- Is there anything he loved, said often, or was known for?

Share whatever comes to mind — even rough fragments — and I'll help you shape it into something warm and personal. There's no wrong way to do this.
