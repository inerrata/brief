You gave me the hero, the goal (free-trial signups), and the audience (ops managers at 50–200-person companies) — so here's a real audit.

## What's not working
- **"The All-in-One Platform for Modern Teams"** — three empty signals at once. "All-in-one", "modern teams" say nothing an ops manager can picture; every competitor says the same. No problem, no outcome, no specificity.
- **"Empower your workflow with our innovative, seamless solution built for scale"** — stacked filler ("empower", "innovative", "seamless", "solution"). Zero concrete claim. An ops manager skims this and learns nothing about what it does or what they'd get.
- **"Learn More"** — the weakest possible CTA for a free-trial goal. It asks for nothing and commits to nothing; it doesn't match the action you actually want (start a trial).

## Why it matters for *this* audience
Ops managers at 50–200-person companies buy on time saved and chaos removed, not adjectives. They need to know in one read: what breaks today, and what this fixes.

## Rewrite (one direction)
- **Headline:** "Run ops for your whole company from one place — without the spreadsheet sprawl."
- **Subhead:** "[Product] connects [the 2–3 tools/processes you replace] so your team stops switching tabs and chasing updates. Set up in an afternoon."
- **Primary CTA:** "Start free — no card required"   ·   secondary: "See a 2-min demo"

Swap the bracketed specifics for what you actually replace. Want 3 headline variants on different angles (time saved / consolidation / control)?
