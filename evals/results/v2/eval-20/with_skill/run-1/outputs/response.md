**Core message (one, used everywhere):** *The right local tutor for your kid — vetted, nearby, booked this week.*

## 1. GTM angle
Win the **parent** side with trust and speed (vetted, local, fast booking); win the **tutor** side with steady local demand and easy scheduling. Go **one subject + one city** first so liquidity is real (enough tutors that a parent always finds a match). Lead with the parents you already have on the waitlist; recruit/seed tutors *before* launch so day-one searches succeed.

## 2. Launch email (to the waitlist)
**Subject:** Your local tutor is ready when you are
**Preview:** We're live in [City] — find and book a vetted tutor this week.

Hi [First name],
You joined to find a tutor you can actually trust. We're now live in [City] with vetted local tutors in [subject] — most can start this week.

Tell us the subject and grade, and we'll show you matched tutors with real reviews. Booking takes a few minutes.

**[Find my tutor →]**

— The [Marketplace] team
*(Tutor-side gets its own variant: "Local students are searching now — claim your profile.")*

## 3. First-30-days measurement
North star: **activated matches** (parent books a tutor). Watch both sides of liquidity:
- Parent: signup → search → booking conversion; time-to-first-match.
- Tutor: profiles live, % of searches that return ≥3 tutors (supply health).
- Repeat-booking rate (early retention signal).

Don't optimize vanity counts (waitlist size, signups, page views) — a big list with no bookings is failure. And treat channel attribution as **directional**: in a two-sided launch, word-of-mouth and offline overlap muddy credit, so sanity-check against overall booking growth rather than any single attributed number.
