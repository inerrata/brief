*Assuming: a company newsletter meant to nurture leads/customers toward revenue or retention, not just reach. Adjust if the goal is pure brand awareness.*

Measure a **focused** set tied to what the newsletter is actually for — not everything your ESP reports.

**The ones that matter (outcome metrics)**
- **Click-through rate (CTR)** — are people acting, not just opening? Better health signal than opens.
- **Conversion / downstream action** — signups, replies, demo requests, or purchases attributed to newsletter clicks. *This is the one tied to the business goal.*
- **List growth net of churn** — subscribers gained minus unsubscribes/spam.
- **Retention/engagement over time** — % of list active in the last N sends (are you keeping them?).

**Watch, don't optimize (vanity metrics)**
- **Open rate** — inflated by Apple Mail Privacy and easy to game with clickbait subjects; use as a loose trend, not a target.
- Raw subscriber count and impressions feel good but don't pay rent. Don't chase them at the expense of clicks, conversions, and retention.

**A caveat on attribution:** newsletter influence is hard to credit cleanly — readers often convert later through another channel, so last-click under- or over-counts it. Treat these numbers as **directional**; sanity-check with the trend over time and the occasional reply/survey rather than trusting one attributed number.
