*Assuming: audience = casual houseplant owners who keep accidentally killing plants; goal = get them to try the app when you mention it. Adjust if wrong.*

The trick: stop pitching "a reminder app" (a feature) and start naming the feeling — guilt over a dead plant, the win of one that thrives. Lead with their problem, not your notifications.

Three angles, genuinely different:

1. **Guilt-relief / outcome:** "It's the reason my plants are finally still alive — it tells me exactly when to water, so I stop guessing and stop killing them."
2. **Effortless / low-commitment:** "I do nothing but glance at my phone. It knows each plant's schedule, so caring for them takes ten seconds a week."
3. **Identity / pride:** "I went from serial plant-killer to the person with the jungle apartment. This is the thing that flipped it."

Pick the one that matches who you're talking to: angle 1 for the anxious beginner, angle 3 for the aspirational type. Want these as one-liners for your app store blurb or social bio?
