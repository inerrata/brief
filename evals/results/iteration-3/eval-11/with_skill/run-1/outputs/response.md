## ICP: "The solo service provider who hates admin"

**Firmographics**
- Business size: 1–5 people (freelancers, solo consultants, small agencies, tradespeople).
- Revenue: ~$30k–$300k/yr.
- Industry: services where you bill by project/hour — design, dev, consulting, trades, coaching.
- Geography/setup: digital-first, often no dedicated finance person.

**Decision role**
- The owner *is* the buyer, user, and payer — no procurement, no committee. Low price sensitivity if it clearly saves time; high sensitivity to setup friction.

**Goals**
- Get paid faster and with less chasing.
- Look professional to clients.
- Spend minutes, not hours, on invoicing/admin.

**Pains**
- Manual invoices in Word/spreadsheets; inconsistent, error-prone.
- Late payments and awkward follow-ups.
- Existing tools feel bloated (built for accountants, not them).

**Objections**
- "I already have a free template / my bank does this."
- "Switching is a hassle."
- "Is my financial data safe?"

**Buying triggers**
- Got stiffed or paid very late on a big invoice.
- Crossed a revenue/volume threshold where manual stops scaling.
- Tax season; an accountant told them to get organized.
- A client asked for a "proper" invoice.

**Watering holes**
- Freelancer subreddits and Facebook groups, Indie Hackers, niche trade forums.
- YouTube/TikTok "freelance finance" creators.
- Accounting-tool marketplaces and integrations (e.g. wherever they already do books).

**Messaging implication:** lead on "get paid faster, look pro, in minutes" — not feature lists. Differentiate against FreshBooks/Wave on *simplicity and speed to first invoice*.
