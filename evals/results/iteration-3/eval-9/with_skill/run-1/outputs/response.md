## One-page launch brief

- **Audience:** people who keep starting habits and dropping them — want a tracker that makes consistency feel achievable, not another guilt machine.
- **Core message (one):** "Build habits that actually stick — because the app adapts to your real life, not an ideal one."
- **Primary metric:** week-1 activation (% who log a habit 4+ days in first 7). Secondary: installs, D30 retention.

## Phased plan

**Pre-launch (weeks −4 to 0)**
- Build a waitlist landing page; collect emails with a "founding user" hook.
- Seed 5–10 beta testers; capture 2–3 real testimonials and before/after stories.
- Warm channels: founder posting the build journey on LinkedIn/X; line up 3–5 niche newsletters/communities.

**Launch (week 0)**
- Coordinated day: Product Hunt + app store live + waitlist email blast + founder posts.
- Limited "founding user" perk (lifetime discount / badge) to drive urgency.

**Post-launch (weeks 1–4)**
- Lifecycle email sequence (see below) to convert installs → activated.
- Double down on the channel that drove the best activation, not the most installs.
- Weekly metric review; ship one retention improvement based on where users drop.

## Lifecycle email sequence (copy module)
1. **Welcome** — "Pick one habit. Just one." (subject: *Start with one*) → set first habit.
2. **Day 2** — reinforce the streak; tiny win framing.
3. **Day 5** — social proof + "you're 4 days in, here's what happens at day 21."
4. **Day 10 (lapsed branch)** — win-back if no activity: "Missed a day? That's the whole point of this app."
