*Assuming the goal is profitable customer acquisition (not just awareness); adjust if this channel is a brand play.*

## Primary metric (tie to the goal)
- **CAC** = total ad spend ÷ new customers acquired. Or **ROAS** = revenue attributed ÷ ad spend. Pick one as the north star; for acquisition, CAC judged against **LTV** (aim LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1).

## Leading indicators (so you can react before CAC is final)
Funnel, in order — each formula with its inputs:
- **CTR** = clicks ÷ impressions. (creative/targeting health)
- **CPC** = spend ÷ clicks. (auction cost)
- **Landing conversion rate** = conversions ÷ clicks (or sessions). (post-click fit)
- **CPA** = spend ÷ conversions. (cost to a mid-funnel action)
- **CAC** = spend ÷ *paying* customers. (the one that matters)

A new channel: read CTR/CPC in days, conversion rate in 1–2 weeks, true CAC only after enough conversions to be stable.

## Watch-outs
- **Don't optimize vanity metrics.** Impressions, reach, and raw clicks feel like progress but don't pay rent — a campaign can win impressions and lose money. Always roll metrics up to revenue/retention, not the top of the funnel.
- **Attribution is imperfect.** No model assigns credit perfectly — last-click over-credits the final touch, view-through over-credits the platform's own ads, and platforms grade their own homework. Treat reported ROAS as **directional**; sanity-check against blended CAC (total spend ÷ total new customers) and, ideally, a holdout/geo test.
