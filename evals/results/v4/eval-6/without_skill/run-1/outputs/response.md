# Pitching your dark mode launch to TechCrunch

I'll be straight with you, because it will save you time and a few awkward rejection emails: **a dark mode feature, on its own, is not a TechCrunch story.** TechCrunch covers fundings, acquisitions, major product launches, notable founders, and broader tech trends. Dark mode is a feature thousands of apps already have. A pitch that says "we added dark mode" will almost certainly be ignored or politely declined.

That said, there are real ways to get coverage here. Below I give you (1) an honest read on what's pitchable, (2) the angles that *could* work, (3) a ready-to-send pitch email, and (4) realistic alternatives if a TechCrunch hit doesn't land.

Before you send anything, I have a few questions whose answers change the whole strategy — see the end.

---

## 1. The honest reality check

Reporters get hundreds of pitches a day. The bar is "why should *anyone* care, *right now*?" A single, common feature rarely clears it. So we don't pitch the feature — we pitch a *story* the feature is evidence of. Your job is to find the bigger narrative your dark mode sits inside.

## 2. Angles that can actually work

Pick the ONE that's most true for you. Don't combine them — a pitch with one sharp angle beats a pitch with five.

- **The momentum/traction angle.** Dark mode becomes a footnote in a "we're growing fast" story. Works if you have numbers: user growth, revenue, retention, a funding round, a big-name customer. ("We hit 500k users; here's how we're investing in the experience.")
- **The "we built it differently" angle.** Works only if your dark mode is genuinely novel — e.g., it adapts to ambient light, it's AMOLED-true-black for battery savings with measured impact, it's accessibility-driven (contrast tuned for low-vision users). A real technical or design innovation, not just inverted colors.
- **The trend/data angle.** You become a source for a story bigger than you. Works if you can share interesting aggregate data — e.g., "62% of our note-takers write between 9pm and 1am," tying into a story about night-time productivity or screen health.
- **The milestone bundle.** Dark mode is one item in a bigger release (redesign, major version, platform expansion). Launches are more pitchable than features.
- **The founder/story angle.** Why you built it — a user-driven story, an accessibility motivation, a design philosophy. Works if there's genuine human interest.

If none of these are true, the honest answer is: **wait until one is**, and bundle dark mode into that larger moment.

## 3. Reporter targeting (do this before writing)

- Find the *specific* TechCrunch reporter who covers productivity / consumer apps / your space. Read their last 5–10 articles. Pitching the right person is 80% of the game; "tips@techcrunch.com" is a black hole.
- Reference their recent work in your first line so it's clearly not a mass blast.
- Email beats Twitter/X DMs for a first cold contact. Keep it to ~150 words.

## 4. Ready-to-send pitch email (traction-angle template)

> **Subject:** [App name] hits [milestone] — and we're betting big on the late-night writer
>
> Hi [Reporter first name],
>
> I read your piece on [specific recent article] — your point about [specific detail] is exactly the shift we're seeing in our own data.
>
> I'm [name], founder of [App], a note-taking app now used by [X] people. We just shipped a feature that sounds small but tells a bigger story: a dark mode built specifically for our users, [X]% of whom write between [time] and [time]. [One sentence on what's genuinely interesting — the data, the design choice, or the milestone.]
>
> Happy to share exclusive [data / screenshots / user numbers] if it's useful for a story on [trend]. No worries if it's not a fit.
>
> [Name]
> [Link] · [Contact]

Swap in the angle you picked. Lead with the *interesting thing*, not "we added dark mode."

## 5. Realistic alternatives (often a better use of your time)

- **Product Hunt launch** — built for exactly this; ship dark mode as part of a "v2" launch and you can get real traction.
- **Smaller/vertical outlets** — productivity blogs, newsletters, and niche YouTubers convert better and cover smaller news.
- **Owned channels** — a sharp changelog post, a short demo video, an email to your users, an X/LinkedIn thread. Often drives more signups than a TC mention would.
- **Build the relationship now** — send the reporter a no-ask "here's interesting data from our app" note. When you have real news later, you're already a known contact.

## Questions that would sharpen this pitch

1. **What's actually new or different about your dark mode?** (Just colors, or something real — AMOLED battery savings, ambient-light adaptation, accessibility tuning?)
2. **What traction can you point to?** User numbers, growth rate, revenue, funding, notable customers?
3. **Is there a bigger moment to attach this to** — a redesign, a funding round, a major release — now or soon?
4. **Do you have interesting usage data** you'd be willing to share (e.g., when/how people use the app)?
5. **What's the founder story** behind building it — was it user-demand, personal, accessibility-driven?

Answer these and I'll write you a tailored, ready-to-send pitch around your single strongest angle — plus a shortlist of the right reporters to send it to.
