# Starting Paid Ads for Your $49/mo PM SaaS

Great goal. The honest answer up front: **ads work best once you already have a converting funnel and a known customer.** If you turn on ads cold, you will mostly buy expensive lessons. So the "where do I begin" answer is partly "before the ads themselves." Here is a sequence you can actually follow.

A few assumptions I am making (correct me if wrong): you have a live product and a website, you are selling self-serve (credit card, no sales team), and you have a limited budget (let us plan around $1,000-$3,000/month to start). I will flag where the plan changes if any of these are off.

---

## Step 0: Do the math before you spend a dollar

At $49/mo, your unit economics are tight, so know your numbers first.

- **Customer LTV.** If a customer stays ~12 months on average, LTV is roughly $588 (gross). After payment fees, support, and hosting, your contribution margin might be ~$400-$450. That is the ceiling on what you can afford to acquire a customer.
- **Target CAC.** A common rule is LTV:CAC of 3:1, so aim for CAC around $150-$200 to start. If you cannot acquire under ~$200, paid ads will not be profitable yet.
- **Trial-to-paid math.** If 25% of trials convert, every paid customer requires 4 trials. If a trial signup costs you $30 in ad spend, your CAC is ~$120. Track this chain: **cost per click -> cost per trial -> cost per paid customer.**

Write these target numbers down. They are your pass/fail criteria for every campaign.

---

## Step 1: Make sure the funnel converts *before* paid traffic

Ads amplify whatever you already have. If your landing page converts visitors at 1%, ads will be brutal; at 4-5%, ads can work.

- **Have a dedicated landing page** for the ad (not your generic homepage). One clear promise, one CTA (start free trial / book demo), social proof, and an explanation of the specific problem you solve.
- **Reduce trial friction.** No credit card required for trial is usually better for top-of-funnel paid traffic at this price point.
- **Install tracking now:** Google Analytics 4, the Meta Pixel, the Google Ads tag, and ideally a tool that ties signups back to the ad/keyword (even UTM parameters plus your own DB is enough to start). You cannot optimize what you cannot measure.
- **Set up conversion events:** trial signup and paid conversion as distinct events. Feed these back to the ad platforms so they can optimize toward real outcomes, not just clicks.

If your funnel is not converting from *organic/referral* traffic yet, fix that first. Paid traffic is the worst place to discover your page does not work.

---

## Step 2: Get sharp on who you are targeting and what you say

PM software is a crowded category (Asana, Monday, ClickUp, Trello, Notion, Jira...). You will not win on "project management" broadly - you will win on a specific audience and angle.

- **Who specifically?** "Marketing agencies with 5-20 people," "construction subcontractors," "freelance teams," etc. The narrower, the cheaper and more relevant your ads.
- **What is the wedge?** Why you instead of the incumbents? Cheaper, simpler, built for X niche, a specific feature, faster setup. Your ad copy should lead with that.
- **What is the trigger?** What makes someone shop for PM software *today* (team grew, drowning in spreadsheets, deadlines slipping)? Speak to that moment.

This positioning work feeds every ad you write. Skipping it is the most common reason SaaS ad budgets get wasted.

---

## Step 3: Pick ONE channel to start

Do not spread a small budget across five platforms. Start with one, learn, then expand. Your two best first candidates:

**Google Search Ads (usually the best first channel for B2B SaaS)**
- Captures *existing demand* - people literally searching "project management software," "[competitor] alternative," "task tracker for agencies."
- Higher intent -> higher conversion -> easier to prove ROI quickly.
- Start with bottom-funnel keywords: competitor alternatives, "best PM tool for [niche]," and your category terms. Use exact/phrase match, not broad, to control spend.
- Watch for: high CPCs in this category ($5-$15+ per click on competitive terms). Niche long-tail keywords are cheaper and convert better.

**Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Ads**
- Better for *creating* demand and retargeting. Strong for reaching a defined niche audience who is not actively searching.
- Cheaper clicks but lower intent - usually needs more creative testing and a strong offer.
- Great as a **retargeting** channel: show ads to people who visited your site or started a trial but did not finish.

**My recommendation:** Start with **Google Search on a tight set of high-intent keywords**, plus a small **retargeting campaign** (Google or Meta) to recapture site visitors. That combination tends to give the fastest, clearest signal.

(LinkedIn Ads can be excellent for B2B targeting but is expensive - usually too pricey for a $49/mo self-serve product unless your niche targeting is very precise.)

---

## Step 4: Set a budget and a timebox

- **Minimum viable test:** budget enough to get ~50-100 trial signups so you have statistically meaningful conversion data. At, say, $30/trial that is $1,500-$3,000.
- **Daily cap:** start around $30-$50/day on your one channel. Enough to gather data, small enough that mistakes are cheap.
- **Run for at least 3-4 weeks** before judging. Algorithms need a learning period, and you need volume before the numbers mean anything.
- **Decide your kill/scale rule in advance:** e.g., "If CAC stays under $200 after 60 trials, scale spend 20%/week. If it is above $300 with no improving trend, pause and rethink."

---

## Step 5: Launch, measure, iterate

- **Test a few ad variations,** but change one variable at a time (headline, image, audience) so you know what moved the needle.
- **Track the full chain weekly:** impressions -> clicks -> CTR -> cost/click -> trials -> cost/trial -> paid -> CAC. Find the weak link and fix that specific step.
- **Lean on retargeting** - it is almost always your cheapest, highest-ROI spend because those people already know you.
- **Do not optimize for clicks or "cheap traffic."** Optimize for trials and paid customers. Cheap clicks that never convert are the most expensive thing you can buy.

---

## A realistic first-30-days plan

1. **Week 0:** Confirm unit economics (Step 0), set up tracking/pixels/conversion events, build/clean up your landing page.
2. **Week 1:** Launch a tight Google Search campaign (10-20 high-intent keywords, exact/phrase match) at ~$40/day plus a small retargeting campaign.
3. **Weeks 2-3:** Let it run, fix obvious leaks (bad keywords, weak landing page steps), test 2-3 ad variations.
4. **Week 4:** Review the full funnel against your target CAC. Scale what works, cut what does not, then consider adding a second channel.

---

## The one thing to remember

**Ads do not fix a broken funnel - they expose it.** The biggest wins usually come not from clever ad targeting but from a sharper landing page, a clearer offer, and a smoother trial. Get one channel profitable and predictable before you expand.
