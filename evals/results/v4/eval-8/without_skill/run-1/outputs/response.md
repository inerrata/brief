# Your Influencer Campaign Playbook (New Skincare Brand)

Great timing — skincare is one of the strongest categories for influencer marketing because the product is visual, results-driven, and trust-dependent (people want to see real skin, not stock photos). Below is a complete, ready-to-run plan. I''ve made reasonable assumptions where I had no details and flagged the few things I genuinely need from you at the end.

**Working assumptions** (correct me if any are off): a DTC brand selling online (Shopify or similar), a small-to-mid launch budget, 1–4 hero SKUs, and a goal of driving first sales + building social proof rather than pure awareness. The plan scales up or down with your budget.

---

## 1. Pick the campaign''s primary goal first

Don''t run influencers for "exposure." Pick ONE primary objective — it changes who you recruit and how you measure:

| Goal | Best influencer tier | Primary metric |
|---|---|---|
| Drive first sales / revenue | Micro + affiliate creators | Sales via code/link, ROAS |
| Build trust & social proof (UGC) | Micro + nano | # of authentic posts, saved content |
| Awareness / reach for launch | Mid-tier + a hero macro | Reach, impressions, follower lift |
| SEO/review credibility | "Skinfluencer" estheticians, derms | Long-form reviews, search presence |

For a brand-new skincare brand, I recommend **trust + sales as the dual focus**: a base layer of many micro/nano creators generating authentic content and discount-code sales, optionally topped with one credibility anchor (an esthetician or dermatology-adjacent creator).

---

## 2. Choose your influencer tiers (and why micro wins for skincare)

| Tier | Follower range | Typical cost/post | Role in your campaign |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K–10K | Free product–$150 | Volume of authentic UGC, high engagement, cheap |
| Micro | 10K–100K | $150–$1,500 | **Core of the campaign** — best trust + conversion |
| Mid-tier | 100K–500K | $1,500–$8K | Reach boosters, a few per quarter |
| Macro/Celebrity | 500K+ | $8K–$100K+ | Skip at launch unless budget is large |

**Why micro/nano for skincare:** higher engagement rates, audiences that trust their honest "I used this for 4 weeks" reviews, and they''re affordable enough to run 15–40 of them — which gives you a content library and social proof at scale. One macro post rarely converts as well as 25 micro posts for an unknown brand.

**Credibility anchor (optional but powerful):** 1–2 estheticians, "skinfluencers," or derm-adjacent creators who can speak to ingredients (niacinamide, retinol, ceramides, etc.). Their endorsement de-risks the purchase.

---

## 3. Build your target creator profile

Recruit creators who match on:
- **Niche fit:** skincare/beauty/clean beauty/"that girl" routine content — not generic lifestyle.
- **Audience demographics:** matches your ICP (age, gender split, geography where you ship).
- **Engagement rate:** 3%+ for micro, 5%+ for nano (engagement, not follower count, predicts results).
- **Aesthetic fit:** their grid looks like your brand could live there.
- **Authenticity signals:** real comments (not "🔥🔥"), prior brand partnerships that performed, no obvious follower spikes.
- **Skin relatability:** for a new brand, creators who show real skin/texture/concerns out-convert overly polished ones.

**Red flags to avoid:** sudden follower jumps, comment-to-like ratios that look botted, audiences in countries you don''t ship to, creators who post 6 paid promos a week (low trust).

---

## 4. Where and how to find them

1. **Hashtag + keyword mining** on Instagram/TikTok: #skincareroutine, #skintok, #skincareaddict, #cleanbeauty, plus your key ingredient/concern (e.g., #niacinamide, #acneskincare).
2. **Check who already follows/tags competitors** and similar-priced brands.
3. **Your own customers:** email your early buyers and DMs — some are already creators. Cheapest, warmest source.
4. **Creator marketplaces & tools:** Aspire, GRIN, Upfluence, #paid, Insense (UGC), or TikTok Creator Marketplace. Use these once volume justifies the cost.
5. **TikTok Shop affiliate program:** if you sell there, open affiliate so creators can self-enroll and earn commission — near-zero upfront cost.

Build a simple tracking sheet: name, handle, platform, followers, engagement %, niche, contact, status, offer, code/link, deliverables, due date, performance.

---

## 5. Compensation models (mix these)

- **Gifting (product-only):** great for nano + warm fans; expect ~20–40% to post. Cheapest way to seed UGC.
- **Affiliate / commission:** unique code or link, 10–20% commission. Aligns incentives, low risk, scales infinitely.
- **Flat fee:** for guaranteed posts from proven micro/mid creators. Negotiate; rate cards are starting points.
- **Hybrid (recommended):** small flat fee + affiliate commission + free product. Best performance-to-cost balance.
- **Whitelisting / usage rights:** pay extra (often +20–50%) for the right to run their content as paid ads — this is often the highest-ROI add-on, because their authentic content becomes your best-performing ad creative.

---

## 6. Sample budget allocations

**Lean launch (~$2,500/mo)**
- 20 nano/micro on gifting + 15% affiliate: ~$600 in product
- 4 micro on hybrid ($250 fee each): $1,000
- Whitelisting/paid amplification of top 2 posts: $700
- Tools/sample shipping/buffer: $200

**Growth (~$10,000/mo)**
- 30 gifting + affiliate: ~$1,500 product
- 10 micro hybrid (~$500 each): $5,000
- 1 credibility anchor (esthetician): $1,500
- Whitelisting + paid amplification: $1,500
- Platform/tools: $500

Rule of thumb: spend ~30–50% of the budget *amplifying* the best organic posts as paid ads. That''s where new skincare brands get outsized returns.

## 7. Campaign brief & creative direction

Give every creator a one-page brief (loose enough to keep their authentic voice):

- **Brand snapshot:** what you make, who it''s for, the one-line promise.
- **Hero product + the ONE key benefit** to emphasize (e.g., "visibly calmer skin in 2 weeks").
- **Must-mention points:** 1–2 ingredients/claims, price, where to buy.
- **The hook angle:** "my honest 2-week review," "my a.m. skincare routine," "what finally helped my [concern]," "get ready with me."
- **Do''s:** show the texture/application, show real results, on-camera reaction, swipe-up/link in bio + code.
- **Don''ts:** unapproved medical claims, comparing to specific competitors, anything FTC-noncompliant.
- **Deliverables:** e.g., 1 in-feed Reel/TikTok + 2 stories with link sticker; or 1 UGC video delivered as a file for your ads.
- **FTC compliance (required):** clear #ad or "paid partnership" disclosure on every paid/gifted post. Protects you and them.

---

## 8. Tracking & measurement

Give each creator a **unique discount code AND a unique tracked link** (UTMs or affiliate platform). Then measure:

- **Primary:** sales/revenue per creator, ROAS, CAC vs. your other channels.
- **Engagement:** ER, saves, shares (saves matter most for skincare — signals purchase intent).
- **Content value:** how many usable UGC assets you got for ads.
- **Reach/awareness:** impressions, follower growth, branded-search lift.

Tag top performers and re-invite them — a stable of 5–10 reliable creators beats constantly recruiting new ones.

---

## 9. 6-week rollout timeline

- **Week 1 — Setup:** finalize goal, ICP, budget, hero SKU, brief, codes/links, tracking sheet, affiliate program.
- **Week 2 — Recruit:** source 60–100 creators, DM/email outreach, ship product to nano gifting batch.
- **Week 3 — Negotiate & onboard:** lock micro deals, send briefs + products, set post dates.
- **Week 4 — Launch wave 1:** posts go live, engage in comments, capture all content, watch codes.
- **Week 5 — Amplify:** whitelist/boost top 2–3 posts as paid ads, retarget engagers.
- **Week 6 — Measure & double down:** review ROAS per creator, re-sign winners, plan next wave.

---

## 10. Common mistakes to avoid

- Chasing follower count over engagement and audience fit.
- One big macro post instead of many micro posts (worse for an unknown brand).
- No unique code/link = no idea what worked.
- Over-scripting creators until the content feels like an ad (kills trust).
- Forgetting FTC disclosure (legal + trust risk).
- Not securing usage rights, then having no ad creative.
- Treating it as one-and-done instead of building repeat relationships.
