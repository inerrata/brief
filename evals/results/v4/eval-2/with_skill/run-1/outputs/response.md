## LinkedIn

This is your strongest platform for B2B/management content. The format that wins here is **personal story -> business lesson**, not a summary. Write to the ~210-character "…see more" cutoff — that first chunk has to land a complete, intriguing thought.

**Three rules for LinkedIn specifically:**
- Put the blog link in the **first comment**, not the post body. In-body outbound links suppress reach.
- Short lines, lots of white space, one idea.
- No hashtag wall — 3 relevant tags max, or none.

### Option A — The story post (highest reach)

> Two years ago our remote team was quietly falling apart.
>
> No one said it out loud. Standups got shorter. Slack went quiet by 3pm. People stopped turning cameras on.
>
> We weren't burned out. We were *disconnected* — and those are different problems.
>
> So we stopped trying to fix it with more meetings and started building small rituals instead. Here are the [N] that actually changed things:
>
> 1. [Ritual 1 — one line on what it is + what it changed]
> 2. [Ritual 2]
> 3. [Ritual 3]
> 4. [Ritual 4]
>
> The one that surprised me most: [most counterintuitive ritual + the specific shift you saw].
>
> None of these took budget or extra headcount. They took 15 minutes a week and a decision to be intentional.
>
> Full breakdown of how we run each one in the comments.

*(First comment: "Wrote up exactly how we run all [N] here -> [LINK]")*

### Option B — The contrarian take (invites debate = reach)

> "Remote teams need more team-building."
>
> No they don't. Most virtual team-building is a calendar tax everyone resents.
>
> What disconnected teams actually need is small, repeatable rituals — not a 90-minute Zoom escape room.
>
> Here's the difference, and the [N] rituals we use instead: [thread the 3-4 key ones, one line each]

> Link to the full post in the comments.

### Option C — The carousel (LinkedIn PDF document)

Highest save-rate format. One ritual per slide.
- **Slide 1 (cover):** "[N] rituals that fixed our disconnected remote team" — big text, minimal design.
- **Slides 2-[N+1]:** one ritual each -> name it, one line on how it works, one line on the result.
- **Final slide:** "Want the full playbook? Link in the first comment." + your face/logo so the account is recognizable.

## X / Twitter

Punchy, one idea per tweet, **thread for depth**. The first tweet is the ad for the whole thread — lead with the payoff, not a throat-clear. Native text outperforms link posts, so the link goes in the **last tweet**, not the first.

### The thread

**Tweet 1 (the hook — this is 80% of the work):**
> Our remote team felt disconnected for months.
>
> Not burned out. Disconnected — different problem, different fix.
>
> We solved it with [N] tiny rituals. No budget, no extra meetings.
>
> Here's each one (thread)

**Tweets 2-[N+1]** (one ritual per tweet):
> 1/ [Ritual name]
>
> [What it is in one sentence.]
>
> [The specific change it created — a number if you have one.]

…repeat for each ritual. Keep each self-contained — people quote-tweet individual tweets.

**Final tweet (CTA + link):**
> That's the system.
>
> If your team's gone quiet, start with just one of these for two weeks.
>
> I wrote up exactly how we run all [N] (with templates) here: [LINK]

### Plus 1-2 standalone tweets (don't waste the good lines on the thread alone)

> Unpopular opinion: most "virtual team building" makes remote teams feel *more* disconnected, not less.
>
> A 15-minute weekly ritual beats a 90-minute Zoom game every time.

> The cheapest fix for a disconnected remote team isn't a retreat or a tool.
>
> It's [your single best ritual], done every week without fail.

## Instagram

Visual-first — the **image/video is the content**, the caption supports it. Two formats do the work here: a **carousel** (drives saves) and a **Reel** (drives reach). The first caption line has to hook before the "…more" cutoff at ~125 characters.

### Carousel (the workhorse)

Same skeleton as the LinkedIn carousel, but more visual and less text-dense:
- **Slide 1 (cover = the real hook):** Bold text on a clean background — "[N] rituals that saved our remote team" or "Your remote team isn't burned out. It's disconnected."
- **Slides 2-[N+1]:** one ritual per slide. Big headline word, one short supporting line. Keep it legible at a thumb-scroll.
- **Final slide:** "Save this for your next team meeting." + "Full guide -> link in bio."

**Caption:**
> Your remote team isn't lazy. It's disconnected. (Different problem.)
>
> We fixed ours with [N] small rituals — no budget, no extra meetings, no forced fun.
>
> Swipe for all [N], then save this for your next 1:1 or team meeting.
>
> 1. [Ritual 1]
> 2. [Ritual 2]
> 3. [Ritual 3]
> ...
>
> Full breakdown on the blog — link in bio.
>
> .
> #remotework #remoteteam #teamculture #leadership #management

*(3-5 relevant hashtags, not 30. IG can't do clickable in-caption links — drive to "link in bio," and update your bio link to the post.)*

### Reel (the reach engine — optional but worth it)

15-45 seconds, designed for **sound-off** (on-screen text doing the work). Quick script:
- **0-2s (visual + text hook):** "Your remote team isn't burned out. It's disconnected."
- **Middle:** rapid-fire the [N] rituals, one on-screen text card each, you talking to camera or voiceover.
- **End:** "Which one would help your team? Full guide in bio."
- **Caption:** short hook line + "Save this. Full post in bio."

## How to actually roll this out

Don't dump all of this in one day. A sustainable drip beats a burst:

| Week | Post |
|---|---|
| Wk 1 | LinkedIn story post (Option A) + X thread |
| Wk 1 | Instagram carousel |
| Wk 2 | LinkedIn carousel (Option C) + 1-2 standalone tweets |
| Wk 2 | Instagram Reel |
| Wk 3 | LinkedIn contrarian take (Option B) + reshare your best-performing piece |

A few things that make or break this:
- **Re-form, don't repost.** Never put the obviously-identical asset on all three — platforms suppress cross-posted content. The versions above are deliberately different per platform.
- **Engage in the first hour.** Reply to every early comment; early engagement velocity is what gets you shown to more people.
- **Links go in comments/bio**, never the LinkedIn or X post body.
- **One idea per post.** Each piece carries one ritual or one angle — don't try to cram all [N] into a single short post.
