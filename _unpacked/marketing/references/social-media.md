# Social Media Reference

This is distinct from `copywriting.md` (which covers the craft of a single post) and `content-strategy.md`
(which covers pillars and calendars). Read this when the task is *how a specific platform works* and how to
make something built for it. For exact character limits and image sizes, see `specs.md`.

## First principles

- **The platform is the medium, not just the pipe.** A post is not channel-agnostic content you "also put
  on LinkedIn." Each platform has a native form, and the algorithm rewards content that looks like it
  belongs. Repurposing means *re-forming*, not reposting (see `content-strategy.md` for the repurposing system).
- **Reach is earned in the first second and the first hour.** The hook decides whether anyone stops; early
  engagement velocity decides whether the platform shows it to more people. Optimize both.
- **You're competing with the user's friends, not other brands.** The feed is mostly people the user
  actually knows. Branded content that looks like an ad loses; content that earns its place by being
  useful, funny, or human wins.
- **One post, one idea.** A post that makes three points makes none. Threads and carousels are how you go
  deep — a single post stays on one hook.
- **Account > post.** Consistency, a recognizable point of view, and a reason to follow beat any single
  viral hit. Optimize for the person who sees three of your posts, not one.

## The hook

The first line (or first 1–2 seconds of video) is 80% of the work — it decides whether the rest is seen.

**Reliable hook patterns:**
- **Bold/contrarian claim** — "Stop posting every day. It's killing your reach."
- **Specific number/result** — "We went from 0 to 40k followers in 5 months. Here's the exact playbook."
- **Relatable frustration** — "Nothing makes you feel like a fraud faster than a blank content calendar."
- **Curiosity gap** — "The highest-performing post I ever wrote broke every rule I'm about to give you."
- **"I was wrong about X"** — admission of a reversed belief; high stop-rate because it's honest.
- **Direct call-out of the audience** — "If you run social for a B2B company, this one's for you."

**Hook rules:**
- Front-load before the truncation point (`specs.md` has the cutoffs). The "...see more" line must land a
  complete, intriguing thought — never cut mid-clause.
- Don't bury the hook under throat-clearing ("I've been thinking a lot lately about…"). Open on the payoff.
- The hook must be honest to the body. Clickbait that the post doesn't pay off trains people to scroll past
  you next time. Earn the click, then keep the promise.

## Per-platform playbooks

**LinkedIn** — professional but human; the best-performing content is a personal story with a business lesson.
- Short lines, generous white space, one idea. The first ~210 characters (desktop) earn the "see more" click.
- Formats that work: the personal-narrative-to-lesson post, the contrarian take, the carousel (PDF document),
  the genuinely useful list. Dwell time and comments drive reach.
- Put links in the first comment, not the post body — outbound links in-body suppress reach.
- Voice: confident and plainspoken. Corporate-speak and hashtag walls read as inauthentic and tank engagement.

**X / Twitter** — punchy, one idea per tweet; threads for depth.
- Lead the thread with the payoff (the best single tweet), then deliver. The first tweet is the ad for the rest.
- Replies and quote-tweets are the engagement engine; a post that invites a take travels.
- Native, plain-text posts and images outperform link posts. Put the link in a reply.

**Instagram** — visual-first; the caption supports the image/video, never the reverse.
- Reels are the reach engine; the static feed is for the audience you already have. Carousels drive saves.
- The first caption line hooks before the "...more" cutoff. The cover frame / first slide is the real hook.
- Saves and shares (especially to Stories/DMs) signal value more than likes.

**TikTok / Reels / Shorts** — the video *is* the content; the caption is secondary.
- The hook is the first 1–2 seconds, visual and verbal at once. Design for sound-off (on-screen text), then
  reward sound-on.
- Native, lightly-produced, fast-paced content beats polished ads. Watch-time and completion rate are king —
  make the payoff worth staying for, and keep it tight.
- Trends (sounds, formats) are distribution; use them, but bend them to your actual point.

**Facebook** — older-skewing, group-driven; organic page reach is low. Best for community (Groups) and
events, not broadcast. Native video and genuinely shareable content (useful, emotional, local) travel.

**YouTube** — the title + thumbnail pair *is* the ad and they're written together (see `product-marketing.md`
and `specs.md`). Long-form rewards depth; the first 30 seconds decide retention.

## Formats that travel

- **The thread / carousel** — one hook, then sequential payoff. Best for "how we did X," frameworks, and lists.
- **The personal story → lesson** — a specific, real anecdote that earns a transferable takeaway. Highest-trust
  format on LinkedIn and X.
- **The contrarian take** — challenge a widely-held belief in your space, then back it. Invites debate, which
  is reach. Only run it if you can actually defend it.
- **The genuinely useful list** — "7 things I'd tell a younger version of me about X." Saveable, shareable.
- **Behind-the-scenes / build-in-public** — process, numbers, failures. Humanizes and compounds trust over time.
- **The native short video** — a single idea delivered fast, sound-off-legible. The highest-reach format almost
  everywhere right now.

## Posting cadence & systems

- **Consistency beats volume.** A sustainable 3×/week you can keep for a year beats daily for three weeks then
  silence. Pick a cadence you can hold.
- **Batch and repurpose.** Create in batches; turn one piece of pillar content into many posts across platforms
  (the repurposing system lives in `content-strategy.md`). One long-form idea → a thread → a carousel → three
  short videos → a newsletter section.
- **Best time to post is when *your* audience is on** — start with platform norms (weekday mornings/lunch for
  B2B), then read your own analytics and adjust. Don't over-index on universal "best time" charts.
- **Engage in the first hour.** Reply to every early comment; early velocity drives distribution.

## Engagement & community

- **Reply like a person, not a brand desk.** Specific, warm, occasionally funny. The replies are content too —
  people read them.
- **Ask real questions, not engagement bait.** "What would you add?" after genuine value works; hollow "Agree?
  Drop a 🔥" erodes credibility.
- **DMs and shares are the deepest signal.** Content people send to one person privately is content that
  converts. Design for "this is so you" forwards.
- **Show up in others' comments.** Thoughtful replies on bigger accounts in your space is the most underrated
  growth channel for a new account.

## What kills reach

- **Engagement bait** ("comment YES", "tag 3 friends") — platforms actively demote it and audiences distrust it.
- **Outbound links in the post body** on LinkedIn/X/Instagram — move them to a comment or the bio.
- **Posting the identical asset to every platform** — cross-posted content that's obviously reformatted from
  elsewhere (TikTok watermark on a Reel, "RT" in a LinkedIn post) gets suppressed.
- **Hashtag walls** — a few relevant tags help discovery; 30 tags read as spam.
- **Hook-and-no-payoff** — clickbait that doesn't deliver trains the algorithm and the audience to skip you.
- **All broadcast, no presence** — accounts that only post and never engage stall. Social is a conversation.

## Quality bar

Before posting, check:

1. Does the first line / first second stop the scroll, and land a complete thought before the truncation cutoff?
2. Is it built *for this platform's* native format, not reformatted from elsewhere?
3. One idea, one job — is the post trying to do too much?
4. Does the hook honestly match the payoff?
5. Is there a reason to engage (a real question, a take worth a reply, something worth saving or sending)?
6. No engagement bait, no in-body links where they suppress reach, no hashtag wall?
7. Does it sound like a person with a point of view, not a brand account reading a press release?
